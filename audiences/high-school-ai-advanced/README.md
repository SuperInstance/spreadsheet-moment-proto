**For students who love AI but haven't taken advanced math yet**

- You're fascinated by AI and machine learning
- You've taken or are taking AP Calculus/Statistics
- You want to understand the math behind AI without getting lost
- You're considering a career in AI, data science, or computer science
- You enjoy solving puzzles and understanding how things work

## 📚 What You'll Learn

These papers take the complex mathematical frameworks from SuperInstance and explain them through:
- **Real-world analogies** that make abstract concepts concrete
- **Step-by-step breakdowns** of mathematical formulas
- **Visual explanations** with diagrams and illustrations
- **Hands-on activities** you can try yourself
- **AI career connections** showing why this matters

## 🧠 Learning Approach

### Before You Start
1. **Review Prerequisites**: Make sure you're comfortable with basic algebra and functions
2. **Set Up Your Tools**: Have a calculator, notebook, and computer ready
3. **Create a Learning Journal**: Track your insights and questions

### While Reading
1. **Take Breaks**: Don't try to read everything at once
2. **Do the Activities**: Hands-on practice reinforces learning
3. **Ask Questions**: If something doesn't make sense, write it down
4. **Connect to Your World**: Think about how these concepts apply to apps you use

### After Each Paper
1. **Teach Someone Else**: Explaining to others helps you understand better
2. **Try the Challenges**: Push yourself with the bronze/silver/gold challenges
3. **Explore Further**: Use the provided resources to dive deeper

## 🛠️ Prerequisites

### Math Skills Needed
- Algebra I & II (solving equations, working with variables)
- Basic functions (understanding f(x) notation)
- Coordinate geometry (plotting points, understanding graphs)
- Basic probability (understanding chance and likelihood)

### Nice to Have (But Not Required)
- AP Statistics (helps with data concepts)
- AP Calculus (helps with change/rate concepts)
- Programming experience (any language)

### Tech Setup
- Computer with internet access
- Spreadsheet software (Google Sheets, Excel, or LibreOffice)
- Text editor for simple coding exercises
- Curiosity and persistence!
