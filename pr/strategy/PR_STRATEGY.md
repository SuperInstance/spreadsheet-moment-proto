# Spreadsheet Moment - Public Relations Strategy

**Version:** 1.0
**Campaign Duration:** 30 Days (Pre-launch through Sustained Momentum)
**Target Media Outlets:** 100+
**Expected Coverage:** 50+ Articles

---

## Executive Summary

**PR Objective:** Generate widespread media coverage that establishes Spreadsheet Moment as the leading AI automation platform for spreadsheet users, driving brand awareness, credibility, and user acquisition.

**Core Narrative:** "The First Spreadsheet AI That Actually Learns" - Position Spreadsheet Moment as the paradigm shift from one-shot AI tools to persistent, learning agents that compound in value.

**Primary Angles:**
1. **The Problem:** Current spreadsheet AI tools are amnesiac - they forget everything after each use
2. **The Solution:** Spreadsheet Moment creates reusable AI agents that learn and improve
3. **The Market:** $24B opportunity in spreadsheet automation
4. **The Technology:** Novel approach combining LLM distillation with persistent agents
5. **The Human Story:** Founders built this to solve their own spreadsheet pain points

## 6. Media Training Outline

### 6.1 Founders Media Training Session (3 Hours)

#### Module 1: Message Discipline (60 minutes)

**Key Messages (Must Master):**
1. "Spreadsheet AI That Learns"
2. "From one-shot calculations to persistent agents"
3. "Compounding value in your spreadsheet"
4. "Full transparency, no black boxes"

**Message Framework:**
- Hook: Grab attention
- Problem: Current AI limitations
- Solution: Spreadsheet Moment's approach
- Proof: Data, testimonials, demos
- Call to Action: Try it free

**Exercise:**
- 15-second elevator pitch
- 3-minute company overview
- 1-minute product description
- 30-second customer story

#### Module 2: Interview Skills (60 minutes)

**Question Handling:**
- Bridging techniques
- Deflection strategies
- "That's a great question, but..."
- Staying on message
- Handling hypotheticals

**Difficult Questions:**
- "Aren't you afraid Microsoft will copy this?"
- "What if ChatGPT adds persistent memory?"
- "Why would anyone pay for this?"
- "Is this just another AI wrapper?"
- "What about data privacy?"

**Preparation:**
- Anticipate 20 tough questions
- Develop key message bridges
- Practice staying calm
- Know when to say "I don't know"

**Exercise:**
- Mock interviews with tough questions
- Video recording and review
- Feedback and improvement

#### Module 3: Demo Skills (60 minutes)

**Demo Best Practices:**
- Start with the problem
- Show, don't just tell
- Use real data/examples
- Keep it simple (3 features max)
- Practice until flawless

**Common Demo Pitfalls:**
- Technical issues
- Too much jargon
- Going off script
- Rushing through features
- Forgetting to highlight benefits

**Exercise:**
- Full demo rehearsal
- Recorded and reviewed
- Iteration and improvement

### 6.2 Spokesperson Guidelines

**Do:**
- Speak in soundbites (15-30 seconds)
- Use analogies and metaphors
- Tell stories, not just facts
- Be authentic and enthusiastic
- Admit what you don't know
- Bridge back to key messages

**Don't:**
- Use technical jargon
- Go off the record
- Speculate about competitors
- Make promises you can't keep
- Get defensive
- Say "no comment"

**Key Talking Points:**
- Market: $24B opportunity in persistent automation
- Problem: 10+ hours wasted on repetitive tasks
- Solution: AI agents that learn and improve
- Differentiation: Only platform with persistent learning
- Traction: [Latest metrics]
- Vision: AI agents in every spreadsheet

### 6.3 Crisis Communication Training

**Scenarios:**
1. Critical bug on launch day
2. Negative press coverage
3. Competitor launches similar feature
4. Data privacy concerns raised
5. Customer churn story

**Response Framework:**
- Acknowledge quickly
- Be transparent
- Explain what happened
- Detail the fix
- Commit to improvement

**Exercise:**
- Role-play crisis scenarios
- Develop response statements
- Practice delivery

## 8. Crisis Communication Plan

### 8.1 Crisis Scenarios & Response

#### Scenario 1: Technical Issues on Launch Day

**Severity:** High
**Response Time:** <15 minutes

**Response:**
1. Acknowledge issue publicly (Twitter, status page)
2. Provide regular updates (every 15 minutes)
3. Estimate resolution time
4. Compensate affected users (free month)
5. Post-mortem transparency

**Templates:**
- Initial acknowledgment
- Progress updates
- Resolution announcement
- Compensation offer

#### Scenario 2: Negative Press Coverage

**Severity:** Medium
**Response Time:** <4 hours

**Response:**
1. Don't respond defensively
2. Acknowledge valid criticisms
3. Correct factual errors
4. Offer to discuss concerns
5. Focus on improvements made

**Approach:**
- Reach out to journalist directly
- Provide additional context
- Offer follow-up interview
- Demonstrate commitment to improvement

#### Scenario 3: Data Privacy Concerns

**Severity:** Critical
**Response Time:** <1 hour

**Response:**
1. Immediate statement (we take privacy seriously)
2. Transparency about data practices
3. Independent audit offer
4. Security documentation
5. Customer communication

**Key Messages:**
- Data encryption
- SOC2 compliance commitment
- User data ownership
- Local execution options
- Enterprise security features

#### Scenario 4: Competitive Attack

**Severity:** Medium
**Response Time:** <24 hours

**Response:**
1. Don't engage directly
2. Reinforce differentiation
3. Highlight customer success
4. Focus on innovation roadmap
5. Let community defend you

**Approach:**
- Take high road
- Focus on value delivery
- Avoid mudslinging
- Customer testimonials
- Technical superiority
