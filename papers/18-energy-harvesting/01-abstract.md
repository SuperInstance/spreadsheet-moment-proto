# Abstract: Energy Harvesting for Self-Powered SuperInstance Systems

**Author:** Casey DiGennaro
**Affiliation:** SuperInstance Research
**Date:** March 2026

---

## Thesis Statement

We demonstrate that **SuperInstance systems can achieve perpetual battery-free operation through ambient energy harvesting**, enabling AI deployment in environments where power infrastructure is impossible, impractical, or unreliable.

---

## Summary

This dissertation presents a comprehensive framework for self-powered SuperInstance systems that harvest energy from ambient sources to achieve indefinite operation. We develop energy-adaptive computation, intermittent computing architectures, and optimal energy allocation policies that enable AI anywhere.

### Core Contributions

1. **Energy-Adaptive Computation**: We develop dynamic precision scaling and task scheduling that adapts computation intensity to available energy, maintaining operation across 100x energy availability variations.

2. **Intermittent Computing Architecture**: We design checkpoint/restart mechanisms with non-volatile state preservation that guarantee forward progress despite frequent power failures.

3. **Optimal Energy Allocation**: We prove optimal policies for allocating harvested energy across computation, sensing, and communication tasks.

### Key Results

| Metric | Battery-Powered | Energy-Harvesting | Advantage |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Lifetime | 2-5 years | **Infinite** | No replacement |
| Maintenance | Regular | **None** | Deploy-and-forget |
| Location | Near power | **Anywhere** | Environmental monitoring |
| Cost | Ongoing | **One-time** | Zero operating cost |

### Theoretical Innovation

We prove three fundamental theorems:

1. **T1 (Sufficiency Bound)**: Perpetual operation achievable when average harvest exceeds average consumption.

2. **T2 (Progress Guarantee)**: Minimum computation rate guaranteed by duty cycle adaptation.

3. **T3 (Optimal Allocation)**: Energy allocated to highest marginal utility tasks maximizes system value.
