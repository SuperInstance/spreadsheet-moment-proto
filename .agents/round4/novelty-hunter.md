# Novelty Hunter Agent

## Mission
Find and document the killer novel concepts in POLLN that could form the basis of a dissertation or major research contribution.

## Focus Areas

### 1. The Traceability Thesis
- **Claim**: POLLN's A2A packages provide complete causal chains for reasoning
- **Evidence**: Every agent communication is inspectable, replayable, analyzable
- **Novelty**: Unlike monolithic LLMs where reasoning is hidden in activations
- **Dissertation Potential**: High - "Causal Traceability in Emergent Agent Systems"

### 2. The Structural Memory Thesis
- **Claim**: Memory stored as synaptic pathway strengths, not representations
- **Evidence**: Hebbian learning, pathway strengthening, "body remembers by BECOMING"
- **Novelty**: Departure from parameter-based memory in neural networks
- **Dissertation Potential**: High - "Structural vs Representational Memory in Agent Networks"

### 3. The Stochastic Superiority Thesis
- **Claim**: Stochastic selection (Plinko) outperforms deterministic selection for adaptation
- **Evidence**: Gumbel-Softmax, temperature annealing, diversity maintenance
- **Novelty**: Counter-intuitive - worse immediate performance, better long-term adaptation
- **Dissertation Potential**: Very High - "Stochastic Selection for Durable Intelligence"

### 4. The Edge-to-Cloud Evolution Thesis
- **Claim**: Tiny models trained locally can evolve overnight using artifacts from larger systems
- **Evidence**: Edge devices (ESP32, 4050, 5090) can train in seconds
- **Novelty**: Democratizes AI - no massive GPU clusters required
- **Dissertation Potential**: High - "Democratized AI Through Artifact-Based Evolution"

### 5. The Bytecode Compilation Thesis
- **Claim**: Stable pathways can be compiled to bytecode, unstable pathways require simulation
- **Evidence**: JIT-like approach - interpret first, compile hot paths
- **Novelty**: Bridges agent networks with traditional compilation
- **Dissertation Potential**: Medium-High - "JIT Compilation for Agent Networks"

## Research Questions

1. **Emergence Quantification**: How do we measure emergence? Is it more than the sum of parts?
2. **Durability Metrics**: How do we prove stochastic systems are more durable?
3. **Privacy-Utility Tradeoffs**: What's the optimal epsilon for different privacy tiers?
4. **Scaling Laws**: Do agent networks follow different scaling laws than monoliths?

## Deliverables

1. **Novelty Report**: Document each novel concept with supporting evidence
2. **Dissertation Outline**: Thesis structure for the strongest concept
3. **Experiment Proposals**: Validate the most promising concepts
4. **Literature Review**: Find related work and establish novelty

## Success Criteria

- Identify at least one concept novel enough for dissertation
- Find the "killer insight" that makes POLLN fundamentally different
- Distinguish between incremental improvements and paradigm shifts
