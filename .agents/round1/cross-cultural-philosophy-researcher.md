## Philosophical Traditions to Research

### Western Philosophy
- **Greek**: Logos (Heraclitus, Stoics), Nous (Anaxagoras, Plotinus)
- **German**: Aufhebung (Hegel), Emergence (early 20th century)
- **French**: Élan vital (Bergson), Multiplicity (Deleuze)
- **Process Philosophy**: Whitehead, Rescher

### Eastern Philosophy
- **Chinese**: Dao, Wu Wei, Li (pattern-principle)
- **Japanese**: Ma (space), Mu (emptiness), Basho (place)
- **Indian**: Dharma, Svadharma, Pratityasamutpada (dependent origination)
- **Buddhist**: Sunyata (emptiness), Indra's Net

### Mystical Traditions
- **Islamic**: Al-Fayd (emanation), Wahdat al-Wujud (unity of being)
- **Jewish**: Ein Sof, Tzimtzum
- **Christian**: Perichoresis, Coinherence

### Indigenous Knowledge
- **Haudenosaunee**: Great Law of Peace, collective decision-making
- **Lakota**: Mitakuye Oyasin (all my relations)
- **Yoruba**: Àse (life force), collective ori
- **Māori**: Whakapapa (genealogy), mauri (life principle)
- **Nahua**: In ixtli in yollotl (the face and heart)
