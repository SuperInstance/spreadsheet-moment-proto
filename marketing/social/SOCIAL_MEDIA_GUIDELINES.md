## Brand Voice & Personality

### Core Brand Attributes

**Professional but Approachable**
- We're experts but not condescending
- We use clear language, not jargon
- We're helpful, not lecturing
- We're confident, not arrogant

**Example:**
✅ "Here's how to use AI agents to automate your spreadsheets in 3 simple steps"
❌ "You're probably doing spreadsheets wrong. Let us show you the right way."

**Innovative but Grounded**
- We're forward-thinking but practical
- We're excited about AI but realistic
- We promise what we can deliver
- We're visionaries who execute

**Example:**
✅ "Our AI agents can save you 10 hours per week on data analysis"
❌ "Our AI will solve all your problems and change your life forever"

**Global but Local**
- We serve a global audience
- We respect cultural differences
- We're inclusive and welcoming
- We adapt to local contexts

**Example:**
✅ "For our users in Europe, here's how GDPR compliance works with our platform"
❌ "Everyone should use our product the same way regardless of where they live"

**Transparent but Strategic**
- We're open about our product and process
- We share our challenges and learnings
- We protect confidential information
- We're honest about limitations

### Tone Guidelines

**DO:**
- Use active voice ("We're building" not "We are building")
- Write in first person plural ("we", "our")
- Use contractions ("we're", "don't", "can't")
- Keep sentences under 20 words when possible
- Use concrete examples and data
- Show empathy and understanding
- Celebrate customers and community

**DON'T:**
- Use passive voice
- Write in third person
- Use corporate jargon ("synergy", "leverage", "paradigm shift")
- Make assumptions about user knowledge
- Overpromise or exaggerate
- Use exclamation points excessively (!!!)
- Be defensive when receiving feedback

### Voice Variations by Context

**Customer Support:**
- Tone: Empathetic, patient, solution-oriented
- Focus: Helping users solve problems
- Language: Clear, step-by-step instructions

**Example:**
"I understand how frustrating that can be. Let's get this fixed for you right away. Here's what we'll do..."

**Product Updates:**
- Tone: Excited, informative, transparent
- Focus: Sharing value and improvements
- Language: Benefit-driven, specific

**Example:**
"We're excited to announce AI Agent Autopilot! This feature monitors your spreadsheets 24/7 and alerts you to important changes."

**Thought Leadership:**
- Tone: Authoritative but humble, insightful
- Focus: Sharing expertise and perspective
- Language: Professional, evidence-based

**Example:**
"After analyzing 1,000+ spreadsheet workflows, we've identified three patterns that separate high-performing teams from the rest."

**Community Engagement:**
- Tone: Welcoming, appreciative, fun
- Focus: Building relationships
- Language: Casual, friendly, inclusive

**Example:**
"Love seeing what you're building! This is exactly why we open-sourced our platform. Keep up the amazing work!"

## Content Standards

### Quality Standards

**All Content Must:**
- Be accurate and factually correct
- Provide clear value to the audience
- Align with brand voice and values
- Be free of typos and grammatical errors
- Include appropriate calls-to-action
- Comply with platform guidelines
- Respect copyright and intellectual property

**Content Review Checklist:**
- [ ] Accurate information
- [ ] Clear value proposition
- [ ] On-brand voice and tone
- [ ] Error-free grammar and spelling
- [ ] Appropriate call-to-action
- [ ] Platform-optimized formatting
- [ ] Visual elements included (if applicable)
- [ ] Links tested and working
- [ ] Accessibility compliant (alt text, captions)
- [ ] Legal and compliance approved

### Visual Standards

**Image Guidelines:**
- Use high-resolution images (minimum 1080p for video, 1500px for images)
- Include alt text for accessibility
- Use brand colors: Blue (#0066CC), Green (#00CC66)
- Maintain consistent style across platforms
- Include logo or branding where appropriate

**Video Guidelines:**
- Use high-quality audio (clear, no background noise)
- Include captions or subtitles
- Optimize for mobile viewing (vertical or square)
- Keep intros under 5 seconds
- Include branding in first 3 seconds
- Add end screens with CTAs
- Optimize file sizes for platform limits

**Thumbnail Guidelines:**
- Use 1280x720px resolution
- Include clear, readable text (under 40 characters)
- Use high contrast for visibility
- Show emotion or action
- Limit to 3-4 elements max
- Test on mobile devices

### Hashtag Strategy

**Platform-Specific Limits:**
- Twitter/X: 1-3 hashtags per tweet
- Instagram: 5-10 hashtags per post
- LinkedIn: 3-5 hashtags per post
- YouTube: 3-5 hashtags per video

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

### Twitter/X Guidelines

**Content Approach:**
- Real-time and conversational
- Thread-based storytelling
- Active community engagement
- Rapid response to mentions

**Posting Frequency:**
- 3-5 tweets per day
- 1-2 threads per week
- Respond to mentions within 1 hour
- Engage with community daily

**Best Practices:**
- Use threads for complex topics (10-20 tweets)
- Include relevant images or videos
- Tag relevant accounts (with permission)
- Use polls and questions for engagement
- RT valuable content from community
- Quote tweets with commentary

**Avoid:**
- Posting more than 6 times per day
- Excessive self-promotion
- Ignoring mentions or DMs
- Political or controversial topics
- Spammy hashtag use (3+ hashtags)

### LinkedIn Guidelines

**Content Approach:**
- Professional and educational
- Long-form thought leadership
- Industry insights and analysis
- Company culture and values

**Posting Frequency:**
- 1-2 posts per weekday
- 1 long-form post per week
- Engage with comments daily
- Share employee posts

**Best Practices:**
- Write 600-1200 words for long-form posts
- Use image carousels for education
- Tag team members and customers (with permission)
- Share data and insights
- Celebrate team and community
- Use professional language

**Avoid:**
- Overly casual language or memes
- Excessive posting (more than 2x/day)
- Political or religious content
- Controversial opinions
- Spammy self-promotion

### YouTube Guidelines

**Content Approach:**
- Educational and comprehensive
- High-production value
- SEO-optimized titles and descriptions
- Community-focused

**Posting Frequency:**
- Launch month: 1 video per day
- Growth phase: 3-4 videos per week
- Maintenance: 2-3 videos per week
- Community tab: 2-3 posts per week

**Best Practices:**
- Create searchable titles with keywords
- Write detailed descriptions with timestamps
- Design custom thumbnails for every video
- Include end screens and cards
- Add closed captions for accessibility
- Respond to comments within 24 hours
- Cross-promote on other platforms

**Avoid:**
- Clickbait titles (misleading)
- Low-quality audio or video
- Missing descriptions or tags
- Ignoring comments
- Inconsistent posting schedule

### GitHub Guidelines

**Content Approach:**
- Technical and transparent
- Developer-focused
- Community-driven
- Collaborative

**Engagement Frequency:**
- Respond to issues within 24 hours
- Review PRs within 48 hours
- Engage in discussions daily
- Release updates weekly or bi-weekly

**Best Practices:**
- Write clear issue descriptions
- Provide detailed PR reviews
- Celebrate contributors publicly
- Share roadmap and progress
- Be transparent about challenges
- Welcome new contributors

**Avoid:**
- Ignoring issues or PRs
- Rude or dismissive responses
- Breaking changes without documentation
- Unresponsive to community
- Closed development process

## Engagement Protocols

### Response Time Standards

**Critical Issues (Product Outage, Security):**
- Response time: <15 minutes
- Public acknowledgment: <30 minutes
- Resolution update: <1 hour
- Follow-up: Within 24 hours

**Customer Questions:**
- Response time: <1 hour
- Complete answer: <4 hours
- Follow-up: Within 24 hours

**General Engagement:**
- Mentions: <4 hours
- Comments: <24 hours
- DMs: <24 hours
- Emails: <48 hours

### Comment Moderation

**Welcome:**
- Constructive feedback
- Questions and discussions
- User-generated content
- Community support
- Diverse perspectives

**Moderate (Remove or Hide):**
- Spam or self-promotion
- Off-topic content
- Duplicate posts
- False information
- Excessive negativity

**Ban (Repeat Offenses):**
- Harassment or hate speech
- Threats or abuse
- Personal attacks
- Trolling or baiting
- Illegal content

### Escalation Procedures

**Tier 1: Community Manager**
- Handles: General questions, feedback, routine issues
- Response time: <1 hour
- Authority: Provide information, moderate content

**Tier 2: Subject Matter Expert**
- Handles: Technical issues, product questions, complex problems
- Response time: <4 hours
- Authority: Technical guidance, product decisions

**Tier 3: Leadership**
- Handles: PR issues, legal concerns, strategic decisions
- Response time: <24 hours
- Authority: Official statements, policy decisions

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Negative sentiment spike (10+ complaints in 1 hour)
- Media inquiry or journalist contact
- Security or data privacy concern
- Legal or compliance issue
- Executive or customer escalation

## Crisis Management

### Crisis Identification

**Potential Crisis Types:**
- Product outage or bug
- Data breach or security issue
- Negative PR or media coverage
- Social media backlash
- Employee misconduct
- Competitive attack
- Legal or regulatory action

### Crisis Response Protocol

**Immediate Actions (0-1 Hour):**
1. Activate crisis team
2. Assess situation and impact
3. Pause all scheduled content
3. Monitor sentiment and mentions
4. Prepare initial response

**Short-Term Actions (1-24 Hours):**
1. Issue public statement (if warranted)
2. Provide regular updates
3. Engage with key stakeholders
4. Monitor and adjust response
5. Document all actions

**Long-Term Actions (24+ Hours):**
1. Complete resolution
2. Post-crisis analysis
3. Update protocols
4. Rebuild trust proactively
5. Learn and improve

### Crisis Communication Principles

**DO:**
- Respond quickly (within 1 hour)
- Be transparent and honest
- Take responsibility when warranted
- Provide regular updates
- Show empathy and concern
- Stick to facts
- Learn and improve

**DON'T:**
- Ignore or delay response
- Minimize or dismiss concerns
- Blame others or make excuses
- Speculate or guess
- Delete negative comments
- Go silent or ghost
- Repeat mistakes

## Legal & Compliance

### Intellectual Property

**Copyright:**
- Only use content we created or have permission to use
- Credit original creators when sharing their content
- Respect fair use guidelines
- Use Creative Commons licensed content appropriately
- Link to original sources

**Trademarks:**
- Use ™ for unregistered trademarks (Spreadsheet Moment™)
- Use ® for registered trademarks (when applicable)
- Respect other companies' trademarks
- Don't use competitor logos without permission

### Privacy & Data

**User Privacy:**
- Never share private user information
- Don't post screenshots of user data without permission
- Respect confidentiality agreements
- Follow GDPR and data protection laws
- Obtain consent before featuring customers

**Internal Information:**
- Protect confidential company information
- Don't share unannounced features or roadmap
- Respect embargoes and NDAs
- Follow insider trading policies
- Protect financial information

### Disclosures & Endorsements

**FTC Guidelines:**
- Disclose material connections (sponsorships, affiliations)
- Use clear and conspicuous disclosures
- Place disclosures at the beginning of content
- #ad or #sponsored for paid content
- Honest opinions and genuine experiences

**Example Disclosure:**
"Thanks to [Company] for sponsoring this video. All opinions are my own."

## Approval Workflows

### Content Approval Matrix

**Self-Approved (No Approval Needed):**
- Routine social media posts (tweets, LinkedIn posts)
- Community engagement responses
- Customer support responses
- GitHub issue responses
- Routine content within guidelines

**Team Lead Approval Required:**
- Long-form content (blog posts, articles)
- Video content and scripts
- Product announcements
- Press releases
- Marketing campaign content
- Influencer partnerships

**Leadership Approval Required:**
- Company-wide announcements
- Crisis communications
- Executive communications
- Financial or legal content
- Major policy statements
- Media statements

### Approval Process

**Standard Approval (1-2 Days):**
1. Creator submits content for review
2. Team lead reviews within 24 hours
3. Feedback provided or approved
4. Content published or revised

**Expedited Approval (<4 Hours):**
1. Creator marks as urgent
2. Team prioritizes review
3. Quick feedback provided
4. Content published immediately

**Emergency Approval (<1 Hour):**
1. Crisis team activated
2. Leadership consulted directly
3. Immediate decision made
4. Content published or rejected

## Compliance & Enforcement

### Policy Violations

**Minor Violations (First Offense):**
- Verbal or written warning
- Additional training or coaching
- Content removal or revision
- Documentation in personnel file

**Major Violations (Repeat or Severe):**
- Suspension of social media access
- Performance improvement plan
- Probationary period
- Potential termination

**Crisis-Level Violations:**
- Immediate suspension
- Investigation and review
- Legal action if warranted
- Termination if appropriate

### Reporting Violations

**Internal Reporting:**
- Report to manager or HR
- Document incident with details
- Provide evidence (screenshots, links)
- Cooperate with investigation

**External Reporting:**
- Follow company whistleblower policy
- Legal or ethics hotline
- Anonymous reporting options
- Protection from retaliation
