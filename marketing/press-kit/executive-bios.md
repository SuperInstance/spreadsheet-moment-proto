# Executive Biographies - Spreadsheet Moment Leadership

## Sarah Chen
### CEO & Co-Founder

**Sarah Chen** is CEO and co-founder of Spreadsheet Moment, where she's on a mission to transform how the world works with data through AI-powered spreadsheets. She leads the company's vision, strategy, and growth.

Prior to founding Spreadsheet Moment, Sarah spent 7 years at Google as a Principal Product Manager for Google Sheets, where she led collaboration features for a product used by 500 million people worldwide. She spearheaded the development of real-time collaboration, offline access, and enterprise security features that became foundational to modern spreadsheet work.

Before Google, Sarah was a Product Manager at Microsoft Excel, where she worked on data visualization and pivot table features. She began her career as a data analyst at McKinsey & Company, where she experienced firsthand the pain points of spreadsheet-based workflows at enterprise scale.

Sarah holds an MBA from Stanford Graduate School of Business and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from MIT. She was named to Forbes 30 Under 30 in Technology in 2025 and is a frequent speaker on AI, product design, and the future of work.

**Quote:** *"For 40 years, spreadsheets have been powerful but limited—requiring technical expertise, isolating users, and excluding billions of people. We're not just adding features to spreadsheets; we're reimagining them as intelligent, collaborative, and accessible tools that empower everyone to work with data."*

## Michael Rodriguez
### CTO & Co-Founder

**Michael Rodriguez** is CTO and co-founder of Spreadsheet Moment, where he leads the company's technical vision and engineering organization. He oversees product development, infrastructure, and technical strategy.

Michael brings 8 years of experience as a Staff Software Engineer at Microsoft Excel, where he was a core contributor to Excel's calculation engine, formula parsing, and web architecture. He led the team that brought Excel to the web and implemented the real-time collaboration engine that powers Excel Online. His work at Excel directly impacted over 1 billion users worldwide.

Before Microsoft, Michael was a Senior Engineer at Google on the Google Sheets team, where he built the initial real-time collaboration infrastructure. He began his career as a Research Scientist at Xerox PARC, working on collaborative document editing systems.

Michael holds a PhD in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University, where his research focused on distributed systems and real-time collaboration. He holds 15 patents in collaborative software, conflict resolution, and distributed computing.

**Quote:** *"Building spreadsheets that scale to 10,000 concurrent users isn't just an engineering challenge—it's a rethinking of how data synchronization works at internet scale. We've invented new algorithms for conflict-free replication that make massive collaboration feel instant and effortless."*

**Expertise Areas:**

- Open source community building
