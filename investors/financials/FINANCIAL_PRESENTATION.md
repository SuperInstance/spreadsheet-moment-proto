**Speaker Notes:**

1. **Subscription SaaS** (primary): Four tiers from free to enterprise. Our freemium model drives viral growth, while enterprise tier drives high ARPU.

2. **Marketplace** (secondary): 30% platform fee on agent transactions. This becomes increasingly valuable as our user base grows and the marketplace matures."

2. **CAC Payback of 2.5 months:** We recover our customer acquisition costs in just 2.5 months. This means we can grow rapidly without burning too much cash.

3. **Gross Margin of 82%:** We keep 82 cents of every revenue dollar after accounting for API costs, cloud infrastructure, and payment processing.

These economics give us confidence in our projections."

**Timeline:**

**Speaker Notes:**

2. **Raise Series B:** Accelerate growth at Month 24-30 when we hit $20M ARR

Our model shows a clear path to profitability without requiring additional capital, but we may choose to raise Series B to accelerate."

### Slide 9: Sensitivity Analysis (2 Minutes)

**Content:**

**What if [Key Assumption] changes?**

| Assumption Change | Impact on Year 3 ARR | Resilience |
|-------------------|----------------------|------------|
| **Conversion: 5% → 3%** | $120M → $72M (-40%) | ⚠️ Sensitive |
| **ARPU: Flat at $15** | $120M → $90M (-25%) | ✅ Resilient |
| **Churn: 5% → 8%** | LTV 9.6x → 6.3x | ✅ Still healthy |
| **CAC: $50 → $75** | LTV:CAC 9.6x → 6.4x | ✅ Still healthy |

**Conclusion:** Model is resilient. Conservative assumptions. Margin of safety built in.

**Speaker Notes:**

"We've stress-tested our model against key assumptions:

1. **If conversion drops from 5% to 3%:** Year 3 ARR drops from $120M to $72M. This is our most sensitive lever, so we're focused on optimizing conversion.

2. **If ARPU stays flat at $15:** Year 3 ARR drops from $120M to $90M. Still healthy, just slower growth.

3. **If churn increases from 5% to 8%:** Our LTV:CAC drops from 9.6x to 6.3x. Still well above the 3x target.

4. **If CAC increases from $50 to $75:** Our LTV:CAC drops from 9.6x to 6.4x. Still excellent.

The model is resilient with built-in margin of safety."

## Q&A Preparation

### Common Questions & Answers

**Q1: "How did you arrive at your growth assumptions?"**
A: "Our growth assumptions are based on:
- **Conversion rates:** Benchmark data from similar SaaS companies
- **CAC estimates:** Actual costs from our beta channels
- **Viral coefficient:** Early data showing 1.3-1.5x
- **Market validation:** 500 surveys, 100 beta users, 68% retention
- **Competitor growth:** Public growth rates of similar tools"

**Q2: "What's your worst-case scenario?"**
A: "Our downside case (5% probability) shows $21M ARR in Year 3, which would likely result in an acqui-hire. This assumes:
- Slower adoption (3% conversion vs 5% base case)
- Early platform competition
- Economic downturn
Even in this scenario, our unit economics remain healthy (6.3x LTV:CAC)."

**Q3: "What if CAC increases as you scale?"**
A: "We've modeled CAC increasing from $50 to $75 as we scale into paid channels. Even at $75 CAC, our LTV:CAC remains excellent at 6.4x (vs 3x target). We also have:
- Organic/viral channels (Product Hunt, referrals)
- Content marketing (builds brand over time)
- Marketplace network effects (reduce CAC over time)"

**Q4: "When will you be profitable?"**
A: "We'll reach break-even at 100K users (Month 18) and profitability at 1M users (Month 24). At that point, we have two options:
1. Bootstrap to profitability (no Series B needed)
2. Raise Series B at $20M ARR to accelerate growth
Our model doesn't require Series B, but we may choose to raise."

**Q5: "What are the biggest risks to your model?"**
A: "Three main risks:
1. **Platform competition (60% probability):** Mitigated by cross-platform advantage, first-mover brand, marketplace network effects
2. **Slower adoption (20% probability):** Mitigated by strong beta metrics (68% retention), market validation (91% frustrated with one-shot AI)
3. **Economic downturn (20% probability):** Mitigated by productivity value prop (automation saves money), freemium model (low barrier to entry)"

## Presentation Tips

### Delivery Best Practices
- **Confidence:** Speak with confidence, you know your numbers
- **Clarity:** Explain complex concepts simply
- **Honesty:** Acknowledge assumptions and risks
- **Story:** Use narrative to make numbers memorable
- **Interaction:** Ask questions, check for understanding

### Visual Design
- **Simple slides:** One key point per slide
- **Large fonts:** Minimum 24pt font
- **Charts:** Use visuals to show trends
- **Colors:** Use colors to highlight metrics (green = good, red = bad)
- **Animation:** Minimal, only to emphasize points

### Timing
- **Total time:** 20-25 minutes
- **Q&A:** 15-20 minutes
- **Total meeting:** 45 minutes max

### Backup Materials
- **Excel model:** Full financial model in Excel
- **PDF summary:** 2-page PDF summary for quick reference
- **Assumptions doc:** Detailed assumptions and sources
- **Scenario analysis:** Detailed scenario modeling
