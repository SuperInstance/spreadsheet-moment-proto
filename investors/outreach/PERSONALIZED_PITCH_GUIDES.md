## Stage-Appropriate Pitches

### Early-Stage Pitch (Pre-Product)
**Focus:** Vision, market size, team, technical feasibility
- Less emphasis on metrics (use projections)
- More emphasis on research and technology
- Highlight team expertise and background
- Emphasize first-mover advantage

### Growth-Stage Pitch (Post-Traction)
**Focus:** Metrics, growth rate, unit economics, scale
- Heavy emphasis on metrics and projections
- Show growth trajectory and momentum
- Demonstrate predictable growth engine
- Highlight path to profitability

## Pitch Delivery Tips

### Opening (First 2 Minutes)
- **Hook:** Start with a compelling stat or story
- **Problem:** Clearly articulate the problem
- **Solution:** Present your solution immediately
- **Traction:** Share early validation (68% retention)

### Middle (15-20 Minutes)
- **Flow:** Tell a logical story (problem → solution → market → team → traction)
- **Visuals:** Use slides to support your narrative (not read from them)
- **Energy:** Maintain enthusiasm and confidence
- **Interaction:** Ask questions, check for understanding

### Closing (Last 5 Minutes)
- **Ask:** Clearly state your ask ($10-15M Series A)
- **Timeline:** Share urgency (closing in 6-8 weeks)
- **Next Steps:** Propose specific next steps
- **Contact:** Provide clear contact information

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Reading slides:** Slides are visuals, not scripts
- **Too much text:** Keep slides simple and visual
- **No clear ask:** State exactly what you want
- **Defensive:** Acknowledge risks, don't hide them
- **Too long:** Respect their time (30 minutes max)

## Virtual Presentation Tips

### Setup
- **Lighting:** Professional, front-facing light
- **Background:** Clean, uncluttered background
- **Audio:** High-quality microphone
- **Internet:** Stable connection (wired preferred)
- **Camera:** Eye-level camera angle

### Delivery
- **Eye Contact:** Look at camera, not screen
- **Energy:** Higher energy than in-person
- **Pauses:** Pause for emphasis (virtual feels slower)
- **Engagement:** Ask questions, check for understanding
- **Backup:** Have phone ready as backup

### Technical Preparation
- **Test:** Test platform (Zoom, Teams, etc.) beforehand
- **Materials:** Have slides accessible offline
- **Backup:** Share slides via backup link
- **Recording:** Offer to record for their partners
- **Follow-up:** Send slides immediately after call

## In-Person Presentation Tips

### Setup
- **Arrive Early:** 15 minutes early to set up
- **Tech:** Bring adapters, clicker, backup devices
- **Handouts:** Printed one-pagers for reference
- **Dress:** Business casual or startup casual
- **Location:** Visit location beforehand if possible

### Delivery
- **Room:** Read the room (energy, engagement)
- **Movement:** Move around, don't stand behind podium
- **Gesture:** Use natural hand gestures
- **Eye Contact:** Scan room, make eye contact
- **Stories:** Use stories to make points memorable

### Follow-Up
- **Immediate:** Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- **Materials:** Attach slides and additional info
- **Questions:** Answer any outstanding questions
- **Next Steps:** Propose specific next steps
- **Relationship:** Build relationship, not just transaction
