## Access Control

### Access Levels

#### Level 1: Public (No Login)
- **Access:** Homepage only
- **Documents:** Public updates, press releases
- **Restrictions:** No access to confidential information

#### Level 2: Prospective Investor (Email Verified)
- **Access:** Selected documents only
- **Documents:** Executive summary, pitch deck, financial summary
- **Restrictions:** No access to detailed financials, legal documents

#### Level 3: Active Investor (Full Access)
- **Access:** All documents (except sensitive legal)
- **Documents:** Full data room (except redacted items)
- **Restrictions:** No access to other investors' information

#### Level 4: Board Member (Complete Access)
- **Access:** All documents
- **Documents:** Complete data room (no redactions)
- **Restrictions:** None

### Security Features
- **Watermarking:** All documents watermarked with investor name
- **Expiration:** Access expires after 30 days (renewable)
- **No Download:** Documents cannot be downloaded (view only)
- **Audit Trail:** All access logged and tracked

## Communication Strategy

### Update Frequency
- **Monthly Updates:** First Friday of every month
- **Quarterly Calls:** Week after quarterly update
- **Board Meetings:** Quarterly (post-investment)
- **Ad-Hoc:** As needed (major news, challenges)

### Communication Channels
- **Email:** Primary channel (monthly updates)
- **Portal:** Document sharing, archive
- **Video Calls:** Quarterly calls, board meetings
- **In-Person:** Annual investor meeting (optional)
- **Slack/Discord:** Real-time chat (optional)

### Tone and Style
- **Transparent:** Good and bad news
- **Data-Driven:** Metrics and milestones
- **Concise:** Respect time (under 5 minutes read)
- **Action-Oriented:** Clear next steps and requests
- **Grateful:** Appreciation for support

## Board Meeting Management

### Pre-Meeting
- **Schedule:** 2-3 weeks in advance
- **Materials:** Send 5 days in advance
- **Agenda:** Clear and time-boxed
- **Pre-Read:** Required reading for all attendees

### Meeting Structure (2 Hours)
- **CEO Update (30 min):** Business, metrics, milestones
- **Product Update (30 min):** Roadmap, features, demos
- **Financial Update (20 min):** Financials, runway, projections
- **Deep-Dive (20 min):** Strategic topic (varies)
- **Discussion (20 min):** Q&A, feedback, next steps

### Post-Meeting
- **Minutes:** Send within 48 hours
- **Action Items:** Clear owners and deadlines
- **Follow-Up:** Track action items to completion
- **Next Meeting:** Schedule next meeting

## Platform Setup Instructions

### Option 1: DocSend (Recommended)

**Setup Steps:**
1. Create DocSend account (https://www.docsend.com)
2. Choose "Fundraising" workspace template
3. Customize branding (logo, colors, domain)
4. Upload all documents (organize into folders)
5. Set access levels (public, prospective, active, board)
6. Enable watermarking (all documents)
7. Set up analytics (track views, time, engagement)
8. Create investor lists (segment by type)
9. Set up email tracking (updates, announcements)
10. Test all features (access, download, tracking)

**Cost:** $100-200/month (startup plan)

### Option 2: Custom Portal

**Tech Stack:**
- **Frontend:** Next.js 14 + React + Tailwind CSS
- **Backend:** Supabase (auth, database, storage)
- **Hosting:** Vercel (free tier available)
- **Domain:** investors.spreadsheetmoment.ai
- **Analytics:** PostHog or Plausible (privacy-friendly)

**Setup Steps:**
1. Set up Supabase project
2. Configure authentication (email/password, magic link)
3. Create database schema (users, documents, logs)
4. Build frontend (Next.js app)
5. Implement access control (role-based)
6. Add document management (upload, view, track)
7. Set up analytics (PostHog/Plausible)
8. Deploy to Vercel
9. Configure custom domain
10. Test thoroughly

**Cost:** $0-50/month (Supabase free tier + Vercel)

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks development

## Best Practices

### Do's
- ✅ Be transparent (good and bad news)
- ✅ Be consistent (regular updates)
- ✅ Be responsive (reply within 24 hours)
- ✅ Be grateful (thank investors for support)
- ✅ Be professional (respect time and attention)
- ✅ Be proactive (share news before they ask)
- ✅ Be data-driven (metrics over stories)

### Don'ts
- ❌ Over-promise and under-deliver
- ❌ Hide bad news (it will come out anyway)
- ❌ Ghost investors (disappear without communication)
- ❌ Surprise investors (major changes without warning)
- ❌ Share confidential info (other investors, customers)
- ❌ Spam (too many updates/requests)
- ❌ Take investors for granted (relationships need maintenance)

## Crisis Communication

### When Something Goes Wrong
1. **Communicate Early:** Don't wait, communicate immediately
2. **Be Transparent:** Share what you know, what you don't know
3. **Take Responsibility:** Own the mistake, don't blame others
4. **Share Plan:** How you're fixing it, preventing recurrence
5. **Ask for Help:** Investors want to help, let them
6. **Follow Up:** Regular updates until resolved

### Example Crisis Email
**Subject:** [Urgent] Security Incident - Update #1

**Body:**
Hi [Investor Name],

I'm writing to share some unfortunate news. Earlier today, we discovered a security incident [describe incident].

**What Happened:**
[Brief, factual description]

**What We're Doing:**
[Immediate actions taken]

**What We Need:**
[Specific ask if needed]

**Next Update:**
[Timeframe for next update]

I take full responsibility for this. We're working hard to resolve it and prevent recurrence. I'll keep you updated as we learn more.

Thank you for your support.

[Your Name]
