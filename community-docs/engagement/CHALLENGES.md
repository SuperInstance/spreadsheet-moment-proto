# Weekly Challenges

Weekly challenges are fun, skill-building activities that help you learn, connect with the community, and earn recognition. Complete challenges to develop skills, win prizes, and build your portfolio!

## How Challenges Work

### Challenge Structure

Each week, we release a new challenge with:
- **Theme** - Focus area (coding, design, data, etc.)
- **Difficulty** - Beginner to advanced levels
- **Requirements** - What you need to do
- **Resources** - Helpful links and templates
- **Prizes** - Rewards for completion
- **Deadline** - One week to complete

### Participation Levels

#### 🌱 Beginner Level
- **Perfect for**: Newcomers and those learning
- **Time commitment**: 2-4 hours
- **Focus**: Learning basics, getting started
- **Recognition**: Beginner badge, community shoutout

#### 🌿 Intermediate Level
- **Perfect for**: Those with some experience
- **Time commitment**: 4-8 hours
- **Focus**: Skill development, best practices
- **Recognition**: Intermediate badge, physical reward

#### 🌳 Advanced Level
- **Perfect for**: Experienced participants
- **Time commitment**: 8-16 hours
- **Focus**: Complex problems, innovation
- **Recognition**: Advanced badge, premium prize, feature highlight

## How to Participate

### Step 1: Join the Challenge
1. **Check current challenges** in Discord `#challenges`
2. **Choose your level** (beginner, intermediate, advanced)
3. **Introduce yourself** in the challenge thread
4. **Get the resources** and templates

### Step 2: Work on Your Entry
1. **Read requirements** carefully
2. **Use provided resources**
3. **Ask for help** in `#challenge-help`
4. **Document your process**
5. **Test thoroughly**

### Step 3: Submit Your Entry
1. **Complete your project**
2. **Prepare submission** (see requirements)
3. **Share in `#challenge-submissions`**
4. **Provide feedback** on others' entries
5. **Celebrate completion!**

### Step 4: Review & Recognition
1. **Community voting** - Like/react to entries
2. **Judge review** - Expert panel evaluation
3. **Winners announced** - Following Friday
4. **Prizes distributed** - Within one week
5. **Showcase** - Winners featured on blog

## Judging Criteria

### Evaluation Dimensions

#### Functionality (40 points)
- Does it work as specified?
- Are all requirements met?
- Is it bug-free?
- Does it handle edge cases?

#### Design & UX (30 points)
- Is it visually appealing?
- Is it easy to use?
- Is it accessible?
- Is the experience smooth?

#### Innovation (20 points)
- Is it creative?
- Does it solve problems uniquely?
- Does it push boundaries?
- Is it original?

#### Documentation (10 points)
- Is it well-documented?
- Are there clear instructions?
- Is the code commented?
- Can others learn from it?

### Tie-Breaking
If scores are tied, we consider:
- Community engagement (comments, reactions)
- Degree of difficulty
- Learning demonstrated
- Help provided to others

## FAQ

### Can I use AI tools?
Yes, but:
- **Disclose use** - Tell us what AI you used
- **Understand it** - Be able to explain your work
- **Make it your own** - Add significant value beyond AI

### Can I submit late?
Late submissions are accepted but:
- **Not eligible for prizes**
- **Still get feedback**
- **Still earn participation badge**
- **Can be used for portfolio**

### Can I work in a team?
- **Beginner**: Individual only
- **Intermediate**: Teams of 2-3
- **Advanced**: Teams of up to 4

### What if I can't finish?
That's okay! Still:
- **Share what you have**
- **Document your learning**
- **Get feedback**
- **Try again next time**

### Are challenges suitable for beginners?
Absolutely! Challenges are designed for all levels:
- **Beginner level** -专为 newcomers
- **Supportive community** - Help always available
- **Learning focus** - Process over perfection
- **No pressure** - Participate at your pace
