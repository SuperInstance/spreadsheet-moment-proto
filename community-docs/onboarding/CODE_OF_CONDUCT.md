# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment

- **Welcoming** language that embraces diversity and inclusion
- **Respectful** disagreement and constructive criticism
- **Focused** on what is best for the community
- **Empathy** towards other community members
- **Graceful** acceptance of feedback and different viewpoints
- **Patience** with newcomers and differing experience levels
- **Collaboration** over competition and ownership
- **Transparency** in decision-making and communication

### Examples of unacceptable behavior

- **Harassment** - Any conduct that creates a hostile environment
- **Discriminatory** language or actions based on identity or background
- **Sexual** language or imagery, or unwanted sexual attention
- **Trolling**, inflammatory, or insulting comments
- **Personal** or political attacks
- **Public** or private harassment
- **Publishing** others' private information without consent
- **Threats** of violence or harm
- **Intentional** disruption of discussions or activities

## Our Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers
- **Clarify** standards of acceptable behavior
- **Respond** promptly to all concerns and reports
- **Enforce** this code of conduct fairly and consistently
- **Document** enforcement actions when appropriate
- **Provide** guidance on acceptable behavior
- **Protect** community members from harassment

### Community Members
- **Respect** the code of conduct and community guidelines
- **Report** violations they witness or experience
- **Cooperate** with moderators and maintainers
- **Listen** and learn from feedback
- **Help** create a welcoming environment
- **Assume** good intentions from others

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:
- All community spaces (Discord, GitHub, forums, events)
- Both online and offline interactions
- All project-related communication and activities

### Private Spaces
Private communication is also subject to this code of conduct. If someone asks you to stop certain behavior in private, you should comply immediately.

### Outside Activities
We generally do not police behavior outside our community. However, serious offenses outside our community may affect participation within it, at the maintainers' discretion.

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

#### How to Report
**Email**: trust@spreadsheetmoment.com
**Discord**: Direct message any moderator
**Confidential**: All reports are handled confidentially

#### What to Include
- Your contact information (for follow-up)
- The specific behavior that was problematic
- The time and location of the incident
- Any evidence (screenshots, links, logs)
- Names of anyone else who witnessed it

#### Response Timeline
- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Initial Review**: Within 48 hours
- **Resolution**: Within 1 week (complex cases may take longer)

#### Confidentiality
All reports are treated with strict confidentiality. Information is only shared with those who need it to address the issue.

### Enforcement Process

#### Step 1: Initial Review
- Moderators review the report
- Gather additional information if needed
- Determine severity of violation
- Plan appropriate response

#### Step 2: Investigation
- Speak with reporter and accused (separately)
- Collect evidence and witness statements
- Assess context and intent
- Determine appropriate action

#### Step 3: Action
- Communicate decision to parties involved
- Implement consequences (see below)
- Document the incident and resolution
- Follow up as needed

#### Step 4: Appeal
- Accused may appeal the decision
- Submit appeal to steering committee
- Provide additional context or evidence
- Committee reviews and makes final decision

### Consequences

#### Level 1: Warning
**For**: First-time minor violations
**Examples**:
- Inappropriate language
- Disruptive behavior
- Minor disrespect

**Actions**:
- Private warning from moderator
- Explanation of why behavior was unacceptable
- Guidance on acceptable behavior
- No public announcement

#### Level 2: Temporary Suspension
**For**: Repeated or moderate violations
**Examples**:
- Repeated minor violations after warning
- Harassment or bullying
- Discriminatory language
- Persistent disruption

**Actions**:
- Suspension for 24 hours to 7 days
- Removal from community spaces during suspension
- Required review of code of conduct
- Public announcement if offense was public

#### Level 3: Permanent Ban
**For**: Severe or repeated violations
**Examples**:
- Severe harassment or hate speech
- Threats of violence
- Doxxing or sharing private information
- Sexual harassment or misconduct
- Illegal activities
- Repeated violations after suspension

**Actions**:
- Permanent removal from all community spaces
- Ban from GitHub organization
- Public announcement if offense was public
- Report to authorities if illegal activity occurred

### Mitigating Factors

We consider these factors when determining consequences:
- **Intent**: Was it malicious or a mistake?
- **History**: Is this a pattern or first-time offense?
- **Context**: Were there contributing factors?
- **Remorse**: Does the person acknowledge and apologize?
- **Impact**: How severely did it affect others?
- **Response**: How did they respond when confronted?

### Aggravating Factors

These factors increase the severity of consequences:
- **Repeat offenses** - History of similar violations
- **Leadership role** - Higher expectations for maintainers
- **Intentional** - Deliberate violation of standards
- **No remorse** - Refusal to acknowledge or apologize
- **Severe impact** - Significant harm to community members
