## Slide 8: How It Works: The Smart Way to Share

**Visual:**
```
    🤖         🤖         🤖
    ↑↓         ↑↓         ↑↓
    │         ↕️          │
    └──────→ 🎯 ←───────┘
          (Everyone agrees!)
```

**Script:**
"So we need a smart way for computers to talk to each other without getting confused! Here's the secret:"

"Instead of just passing messages around, computers take turns being the 'leader.' The leader helps everyone agree on the right answer, kind of like how a referee in a game makes sure everyone follows the rules!"

"This way, all the computers can share what they know, and they can figure out the right answer together!"

**Interactive Element:**
"Who's been a leader or referee in a game before? What was your job?"

## Slide 9: Cool Trick #1: Deadband (Don't Be Bothered by Little Things!)

**Visual:**
```
    Temperature: 70°F, 71°F, 70°F, 71°F

    🤔 "Should I change the thermostat?"

    ❌ No! It's close enough! 😌
```

**Script:**
"Here's a cool trick computers use! Imagine your thermostat at home - if the temperature changes from 70°F to 71°F, should the heater turn on? Probably not! That's too small of a change!"

"Computers use this same trick! They call it 'deadband' - which means they don't get bothered by tiny changes. They only pay attention to BIG, important changes!"

"This saves energy and helps computers focus on what really matters!"

**Interactive Element:**
"Can you think of something that changed a tiny bit but wasn't important? (Like your hair growing a little bit, or the temperature outside changing 1 degree?)"

## Teacher's Notes

### Preparation Needed:
- [ ] Prepare puzzles/challenges for team activity (Slide 18)
- [ ] Print copies of take-home activity (Slide 22)
- [ ] Test any interactive elements beforehand
- [ ] Prepare questions for discussion sections

### Activity Ideas for Slide 18:
1. **Puzzle Challenge:** Give each team a simple jigsaw puzzle (20-30 pieces)
2. **Building Challenge:** Give teams LEGO blocks and ask them to build something specific
3. **Math Challenge:** Give teams a multi-step math problem that requires different skills
4. **Pattern Challenge:** Give teams a pattern recognition puzzle

### Discussion Questions for Slide 19:
- Was it easier to work alone or in a team?
- Did anyone take charge? Was that helpful?
- Did everyone participate equally?
- What made your team successful?
- What would you do differently next time?

### Assessment:
- Participation in discussions
- Engagement with interactive elements
- Quality of teamwork during activity
- Completion of take-home challenge

### Extensions:
- Research different types of AI and machine learning
- Interview someone who works with computers
- Write a story about a team of computers solving a problem
- Design a computer system that helps with something at school
