### Deep-Just-So Analysis: What Does POLLN Really Mean?

At the **just-so level** - the deepest level of explanation that feels satisfying and complete - POLLN represents:

**The Essence**: Information packets that carry potential. Pollen is:
- **Small** (compressed, efficient)
- **Potent** (a grain contains the blueprint for life)
- **Mobile** (travels by wind, by bee, by intention)
- **Generative** (combines with other pollen to create new life)
- **Selective** (not all pollen lands; only compatible combinations work)

This maps perfectly to our **loglines** (behavioral seeds):
- Small: 64-1024 dimensions, compressed
- Potent: Contains the essence of behavior
- Mobile: Shareable across keepers
- Generative: Can be combined (woven) to create new behaviors
- Selective: Only useful combinations are retained

**POLLN as a word**:
- Sounds like "pollen" (the substance)
- Contains "poll" (gathering opinions, distributed decision-making)
- Contains "all" (collective, everyone)
- Five letters, symmetrical-ish, memorable

## Part 3: Knowledge Artifact Names Workshop

### Raw Event Record

| Old | Candidates | Decision |
|-----|------------|----------|
| Log | Trace, Track, Mark, Imprint, Track, Forage Record, Trace | **Trace** |

**Rationale**: A trace is what's left behind. Lighter than "log." Bees leave traces of their visits. The system traces behavior.

### Compressed Behavioral Seed

| Old | Candidates | Decision |
|-----|------------|----------|
| Logline | Pollen Grain, Seed, Essence, Grain, Spore, Capsule | **Pollen Grain** (or just **Grain**) |

**Rationale**: Pollen grains are the compressed essence of genetic information. Portable, potent, can cross-pollinate. "Grain" is simpler but "Pollen Grain" is more evocative.

### Personal Memory Store

| Old | Candidates | Decision |
|-----|------------|----------|
| Logbook | Hive Memory, Comb Memory, Archive, Store, Cache | **Hive Memory** |

**Rationale**: Bees have remarkable memory for locations. The hive collectively remembers flower positions. "Hive Memory" captures both storage and collective nature.

### Reusable Routine

| Old | Candidates | Decision |
|-----|------------|----------|
| Loom | Honeycomb Cell, Cell, Chamber, Comb, Routine, Pattern | **Comb Cell** (or just **Cell**) |

**Rationale**: Honeycomb cells are:
- Hexagonal (efficient packing)
- Reusable (larvae grow, honey stored)
- Structured (regular pattern)
- Shareable (honey can be extracted)

"Cell" is simpler, "Comb Cell" is more evocative of the bee metaphor.

### Collection of Routines

| Old | Candidates | Decision |
|-----|------------|----------|
| Loomery | Honeycomb, Comb, Cache, Collection | **Honeycomb** |

**Rationale**: The honeycomb is the physical structure holding cells. Natural, beautiful, efficient.

### Shared Routine Subscription

| Old | Candidates | Decision |
|-----|------------|----------|
| Loomcast | Scent Trail, Waggle, Dance, Trail, Broadcast | **Scent Trail** |

**Rationale**: Bees leave scent trails that others follow. A loomcast is a trail others can follow to a good routine.

### The Decision Layer

| Old | Candidates | Decision |
|-----|------------|----------|
| Plinko | Waggle Selection, Stochastic Selection, Dance Decision, Plinko | **Plinko** |

**Rationale**: Plinko is a novel contribution. The game show metaphor (bouncing chip) accurately describes stochastic selection. Keep it - it's distinctive.

## Part 7: Open Questions

1. **"Pollen" vs "POLLN"**: Should we use the word "pollen" in documentation or always the acronym?
   - Recommendation: Use "pollen" when describing the substance, "POLLN" for the system

2. **Keeper vs Beekeeper**: "Beekeeper" is more literal but longer. Is "Keeper" clear enough?
   - Recommendation: "Keeper" is fine, with explanation that it's like a beekeeper

3. **Cell vs Comb Cell**: "Cell" is cleaner but could mean table cell. Is context enough?
   - Recommendation: "Comb Cell" for first use, then "cell" in context

4. **Nocturnal Processing vs Overnight Optimization**: Which captures "dreaming" better?
   - Recommendation: "Overnight Optimization" is clearer technically; "Nocturnal Processing" has more poetry
