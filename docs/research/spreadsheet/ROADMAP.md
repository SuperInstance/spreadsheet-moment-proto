# POLLN Spreadsheet Integration - Comprehensive Roadmap

**"From Black Boxes to Glass Boxes: A 52-Week Journey to Inspectable AI"**

---

## Executive Summary

This roadmap integrates the **Reverse Engineering Engine** with all existing POLLN research (Waves 15-18, Breakdown Rounds 2-8) into a cohesive 52-week implementation plan. The vision remains: **Spreadsheet cells that contain inspectable AI agents**.

### The Innovation

**Reverse Engineering Engine** transforms monolithic LLMs into thousands of inspectable "Fractured AI Boxes" - each box representing a discrete reasoning step, attention head, or transformation layer. Users can see, modify, and understand every decision.

### Core Philosophy

> "Not every problem needs a brain. Not every agent needs agency. Break down until you find the simplest form that works, then optimize from there."

## Phase 3: Agent Breakdown System (Weeks 21-28)

### Overview

Systematically convert large, complex agents into smaller, more efficient components (bots, scripts, functions). Optimize cost and performance while preserving agency where needed.

### Week 21-22: Agency Determination Engine

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] 5-level automation spectrum assessment
- [ ] Decision point analysis
- [ ] Cost-benefit calculator
- [ ] Migration pattern detector

**Key Files**:
- `src/agency/AutomationSpectrum.ts`
- `src/agency/DecisionPointAnalyzer.ts`
- `src/agency/CostBenefitCalculator.ts`
- `src/agency/MigrationPatternDetector.ts`

**Automation Levels**:
- **Level 0**: Function (pure computation, <1ms, $0)
- **Level 1**: Script (automated flow, 1-50ms, $0)
- **Level 2**: Bot (rule-based, 50-500ms, $0.0001)
- **Level 3**: Simple Agent (single task, 2-5s, $0.003)
- **Level 4**: Complex Agent (multi-step, 5-15s, $0.01)

**Success Criteria**:
- Classify tasks into 5 levels with >90% accuracy
- Identify 60-80% of tasks that don't need LLMs
- Calculate cost-benefit with >85% accuracy

## Risk Mitigation

### Technical Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Performance issues** | 40% | High | Progressive enhancement, aggressive caching, lazy loading |
| **Browser incompatibility** | 30% | Medium | Feature detection, polyfills, clear requirements |
| **Memory exhaustion** | 50% | High | Hard limits, monitoring, lazy unloading, auto-save |
| **LLM accuracy loss** | 25% | High | Verification protocols, ensemble methods, fallback |
| **Integration complexity** | 35% | High | Modular design, thorough testing, incremental rollout |

### Business Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Microsoft competition** | 60% | Medium | Open source advantage, faster iteration, platform agnostic |
| **Low adoption** | 30% | High | Clear onboarding, demo video, template library, support |
| **Monetization failure** | 20% | Medium | Freemium model, enterprise features, marketplace fees |
| **Community toxicity** | 20% | Medium | Code of conduct, active moderation, positive reinforcement |

### Legal & Security Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Data privacy violations** | 10% | Critical | Local-first processing, clear policy, opt-in, GDPR compliance |
| **IP infringement** | 5% | Medium | Prior art search, original implementation, legal review |
| **Security breach** | 15% | Critical | Security audit, bug bounty, incident response, encryption |
