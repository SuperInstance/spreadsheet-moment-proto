## Critical Insights for POLLN

### 1. The "Logos" Problem Was Correctly Identified

The previous concern about using "LOGOS" as a system name was **well-founded**. Logos is:
- Sacred in Greek philosophy
- Central to Christian theology (Gospel of John)
- Living religious concept

**Decision to change to "Colony Mind" is culturally appropriate.**

---

### 2. Natural Metaphors Require Care

While pollination/bees metaphors avoid some appropriation concerns, they still require:
- Acknowledgment of TEK (Traditional Ecological Knowledge)
- Recognition that indigenous peoples understood these patterns first
- Attribution when using specific cultural concepts

---

### 3. "Universal" Concepts Still Need Attribution

Even concepts like "emergence" or "collective intelligence" that appear across cultures benefit from:
- Acknowledging cross-cultural parallels
- Citing specific traditions
- Avoiding claims of originality

## Red Flags: When NOT to Use a Concept

**Do NOT use if:**
- [ ] The concept is restricted/esoteric and requires initiation
- [ ] The community has explicitly asked outsiders not to use it
- [ ] Using it would cause harm to the community
- [ ] The concept is sacred and would be trivialized by technical use
- [ ] You cannot establish proper attribution
- [ ] You cannot obtain required FPIC
- [ ] The concept is currently being exploited or appropriated
- [ ] Your understanding is superficial
