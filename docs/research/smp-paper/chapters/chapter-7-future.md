# Chapter 7: What's Next

## The Ocean is Rising. Time to Build a Better Boat.

I've been fishing these waters for thirty years. The ocean doesn't ask permission to change. It just does. The seasons shift. The fish move. The weather gets weirder every year.

You adapt or you find another line of work.

AI is like that. It's coming whether we're ready or not. The question isn't whether AI will transform your business, your industry, your life. The question is: will you be steering that transformation, or just along for the ride?

SMP is about steering.

This chapter maps out what's coming. Not sci-fi speculation. This is the roadmap—the practical evolution of transparent AI from where we are today to where we're going.

## NEAR-TERM: What You Can Touch (1-2 Years)

### Real Spreadsheet Integration

Right now, SMP lives in research code and prototypes. That's changing fast.

**Excel Plugin**: We're building an Excel add-in that turns any spreadsheet into an SMP workspace. You select cells, you pick tiles from a library, you chain them together. No coding. No infrastructure. Just AI that lives where your data already lives.

**Google Sheets Extension**: Same story for the cloud. Collaborative teams can build and share SMP systems in real-time. Watch each other's tile chains. Iterate together.

**The Marketplace**: This is where it gets interesting. A tile marketplace where you can buy and sell specialized tiles.

- "Customer Churn Predictor" tile—$49
- "Financial Anomaly Detector" tile—$99
- "Legal Document Classifier" tile—$199

Domain experts build tiles. Regular people use them. The expert makes money. The user gets AI they can understand. Everyone wins.

### Better Interfaces, Not Just Spreadsheets

Spreadsheets are great, but not everyone thinks in grids.

**Chatbot Integration**: Talk to your SMP system in natural language. "What do my sales tiles think about Q4 performance?" The chatbot shows you the relevant tiles, their reasoning, their confidence levels. You can drill down into any step.

**Visual Flow Builders**: Drag-and-drop tile chains for people who think visually. Connect tiles with arrows. See the data flow. Click any tile to inspect its reasoning.

**Code Export**: Developers can export tile chains as Python, JavaScript, or Rust code. Integrate SMP into existing systems. Embed transparent AI in web apps, mobile apps, backend services.

### More Constraint Types

Right now, tiles mostly handle text and numbers. We're expanding:

- **Image tiles**: Computer vision broken into inspectable steps
- **Audio tiles**: Speech recognition, sound classification, music analysis
- **Video tiles**: Motion detection, event recognition, pattern matching
- **Time series tiles**: Forecasting, anomaly detection, trend analysis
- **Graph tiles**: Network analysis, relationship mapping, fraud detection

Each type transparent. Each step inspectable. Each tile improvable.

## MEDIUM-TERM: When Tiles Get Smart (3-5 Years)

### Federated Learning Across Organizations

Here's something that gets me excited as a fisherman who's seen the industry change.

**Your tile learns from my data. My tile learns from your data. Neither of us shares the raw data.**

This is federated learning for SMP tiles. Organizations with similar problems can collaborate without sharing sensitive information.

Example: Five hospitals want better medical diagnosis tiles.

- Hospital A trains on their patient data
- Hospital B trains on theirs
- Hospital C, D, E do the same

The tiles share what they've learned—the patterns, not the patients. Each hospital's tile gets smarter from everyone's experience, but privacy is never compromised.

The result? Better AI for everyone, built on collective wisdom without collective data exposure.

### Automated Tile Discovery

You know how you can spend hours figuring out the right way to break down a problem?

**AI will do that for you.**

Give an SMP system your data and your goal. It explores different tile decompositions automatically.

"Try breaking this into 3 tiles." → "Performance: 78% accuracy"
"Try 5 tiles." → "Performance: 84% accuracy"
"Try 7 tiles specialized by data type." → "Performance: 91% accuracy"

The system suggests decompositions. You pick the one that makes sense for your situation. Maybe you prioritize accuracy. Maybe you prioritize interpretability. Maybe you need it to run fast on limited hardware.

You're in control. The AI is your research assistant.

### Cross-Modal Reasoning

This is where things get really interesting.

**Tiles that understand relationships between different kinds of data.**

- An image tile sees a photo of a product defect
- A text tile reads the customer complaint about that defect
- A time series tile looks at when similar defects occurred before
- A reasoning tile combines all three perspectives

The system doesn't just process each data type in isolation. It understands *connections* between images, text, numbers, timing. It can reason like a human expert who looks at multiple sources of evidence before making a decision.

And you can see exactly how each modality contributed to the conclusion.

### Neural-Symbolic Integration

Pure neural networks (like today's LLMs) are powerful but opaque. Pure symbolic systems are transparent but rigid.

**SMP tiles combine both.**

Each tile has a neural component for pattern recognition and a symbolic component for explainable reasoning. The neural part spots patterns. The symbolic part explains them.

Example: A medical diagnosis tile

- **Neural component**: "This image looks like 12,000 cases I've seen. 87% match to skin cancer patterns."
- **Symbolic component**: "Here are the specific features: irregular borders (present), color variation (present), diameter >6mm (present). According to ABCD criteria, this suggests melanoma."

You get the power of neural pattern recognition AND the explainability of symbolic reasoning. Best of both worlds.

## LONG-TERM: The Future We're Building (5-10 Years)

### Quantum Tile Processing

I know, I know. "Quantum" sounds like hype. But hear me out.

Some problems are fundamentally hard for classical computers. Optimization problems. Certain pattern matching tasks. Parallel reasoning through massive search spaces.

**Quantum tiles will handle these.**

Your SMP system will have access to both classical tiles and quantum tiles. It'll route problems to whichever architecture handles them best.

- Need to optimize a million-variable logistics problem? Quantum tile.
- Need to understand natural language? Classical tile.
- Need both working together? They chain seamlessly.

You don't need to understand quantum computing. You just use the quantum tile when it's the right tool for the job. The transparency is the same—you can see what the quantum tile did, even if the quantum mechanics stays behind the scenes.

### Tiles That Design Tiles

Here's the mind-bending one.

**SMP systems that improve their own architecture.**

Imagine an SMP system that notices: "This 7-tile decomposition is working, but the third tile is often the bottleneck. I'm going to try breaking it into two specialized tiles."

It automatically creates a variant, tests it, and if it performs better, suggests the improvement to you.

Or: "I notice that tasks involving time series data always use a similar 3-tile pattern. I'm going to create a reusable composite tile for that."

The system isn't just learning to perform tasks better. It's learning to structure itself better.

You're always in control—you approve or reject the architectural changes. But the system is proactively finding improvements.

### Transparency by Default

This is the big one. The reason we're doing all of this.

**What if SMP became how AI is done?**

Not a niche approach for people who care about transparency. The default way to build AI systems.

Imagine a world where:
- Every AI decision is inspectable
- Every AI system can explain itself
- Every AI component can be improved without rebuilding the whole thing
- Trust in AI is earned through visibility, not marketing

Regulation becomes simpler because systems are transparent. Debugging becomes faster because failures are traceable. Innovation accelerates because people can build on each other's work instead of starting from scratch.

We're not just building a better way to do AI. We're building a more trustworthy foundation for AI in society.

## THE BIG VISION: The Spreadsheet Moment for AI

### What If Every AI Decision Was Inspectable?

Right now, when an AI makes a decision that affects your life, you can't see how it happened.

Credit decision. Hiring recommendation. Medical diagnosis. Prison sentencing. News feed curation. Loan approval.

You get a yes or no. Maybe a generic explanation. But the actual reasoning? Buried in parameters you can't access.

**What if that changed?**

What if you could click on any AI decision and see the full reasoning chain?

- "Show me why you approved this loan."
- "Show me why you recommended this treatment."
- "Show me why you rejected this applicant."

And what you got wasn't a post-hoc story, but the actual reasoning path the system used?

That's SMP.

### What If Anyone Could Improve AI Behavior?

Right now, if you want to improve how AI works, you need a research lab, millions of dollars, and a PhD team.

**What if my accountant could fix a bookkeeping AI?**

What if a warehouse manager could optimize an inventory prediction system? What if a teacher could tune a grading algorithm?

They don't need to understand the whole system. They just need to understand the tile that handles their specific concern. They inspect it. They see what it's doing wrong. They improve it or swap it for something better.

This is democratization of AI development. Not just using AI—*shaping* how AI works.

### What If Trust in AI Was Earned, Not Assumed?

We're in a crisis of trust. People don't trust AI. They can't see how it works. They can't verify if it's fair. They can't tell if it's safe.

**Transparency changes that.**

When you can see the reasoning, trust becomes a choice, not a requirement. You can verify. You can challenge. You can improve.

This matters for:
- **Regulators** who need to audit AI systems
- **Businesses** who need to defend AI decisions
- **Individuals** whose lives AI affects
- **Society** which is delegating more decisions to AI

Trust through transparency. Trust through verification. Trust through the ability to inspect and improve.
