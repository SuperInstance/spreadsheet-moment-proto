# Test Script: Confidence Cascade Visualizer
**Moderator Guide - Round 12**

## Session Setup
- Open the Confidence Cascade Visualizer link: [superinstance.ai/demos/interactive#confidence-cascade]
- Ensure screen sharing is active
- Start recording
- Read intro script

---

## Introduction Script (2 minutes)

"Thank you for participating in our user testing today. I'll be asking you to use a mathematical visualization tool called the Confidence Cascade Visualizer. Please think aloud as you work through these tasks - tell me what you're thinking, what you're looking at, and what you're trying to do. There are no wrong answers, and this isn't a test of your abilities. We're testing whether our tool is easy to understand and use. Feel free to ask questions if anything is unclear."

---

## Task 1: Initial Exploration (5 minutes)
**Objective:** Assess first impressions and intuitive understanding

### Instructions
"This is a tool for visualizing how confidence flows through a multi-step system. Please take a few minutes to explore the interface. Tell me what you see and what you think this tool might be used for."

### What to Observe
✅ Whether they notice the GREEN/YELLOW/RED zones
✅ How they interpret the color coding
✅ What they think the percentages mean
✅ Whether they try the controls
❌ Do NOT explain anything unless explicitly asked

### Follow-up Questions (if needed)
- "What do you think the green, yellow, and red colors represent?"
- "What happens when you adjust the slider bars?"
- "Can you predict what the final result might be?"

---

## Task 2: Threshold Manipulation (3 minutes)
**Objective:** Test understanding of configuration options

### Instructions
"Now I'd like you to adjust the confidence thresholds. Can you make it so that a confidence level of 75% shows up as yellow instead of green?"

### What to Observe
✅ Can they locate the threshold controls
✅ Do they understand which slider affects which threshold
✅ Can they successfully make the change
✅ How they react to immediate visual feedback

### Success Criteria
- Successfully adjusts green threshold below 75%
- Observes change in visualization
- Shows understanding of threshold logic

### Common Mistakes to Note
- Adjusting wrong slider
- Not understanding "greater than" logic
- Confusing yellow and green thresholds

---

## Task 3: Animation Understanding (3 minutes)
**Objective:** Evaluate comprehension of dynamic processes

### Instructions
"Please run the cascade animation. Watch what happens and tell me what you think is going on."

### What to Observe
✅ Whether they notice the step-by-step progression
✅ If they understand confidence accumulates/reduces
✅ Whether the speed is appropriate
✅ If the animation enhances or hinders understanding

### Follow-up Questions
- "What do you think the moving indicator represents?"
- "How is the final confidence calculated?"
- "What would happen if one step had very low confidence?"

---

## Task 4: Composition Types (4 minutes)
**Objective:** Test understanding of different cascade modes

### Instructions
"Now try switching to the different composition types - sequential, parallel, and conditional. How do you think these modes are different?"

### What to Observe
✅ Can they locate the mode switcher
✅ Do they notice changes in the visualization
✅ Can they explain the conceptual differences
✅ Whether parallel mode weight controls make sense

### Success Criteria
- Successfully switches between all three modes
- Notices visual changes
- Can articulate at least one difference
- Understands weight concept in parallel mode

### If They Struggle
- "What do you think 'sequential' might mean?"
- "How might parallel processing work?"

---

## Task 5: Real-World Application (3 minutes)
**Objective:** Capture transfer of understanding

### Instructions
"Imagine you're designing a security system that verifies passwords. How might you use this tool or these concepts in that system?"

### What to Observe
✅ Ability to map concepts to new context
✅ Understanding of confidence accumulation
✅ Creative application of knowledge
✅ Relevance of their example

---

## Task 6: Final Reflection (2 minutes)
**Objective:** Capture overall impressions

### Questions
1. "On a scale of 1-5, how easy was this tool to understand?"
2. "What was the most confusing part?"
3. "What did you like best about it?"
4. "Who do you think would find this tool useful?"
5. "Any suggestions for improvement?"

---

## Scoring Guide

### Comprehension Score
- **Excellent (5):** Understands zones, can explain cascade logic, predicts outcomes
- **Good (4):** Gets zones and basic concept, minor gaps
- **Fair (3):** Understands zones but struggles with transitions
- **Poor (2):** Confused by zones or threshold logic
- **Very Poor (1):** Cannot explain any aspect correctly

### Usability Score
- **Excellent (5):** Navigate easily, no assistance needed, finds all features
- **Good (4):** Mostly independent, one minor assist
- **Fair (3):** Needs 2-3 assists, figures out eventually
- **Poor (2):** Multiple assists, still struggles
- **Very Poor (1):** Cannot complete without constant help

### Engagement Score
- **Excellent (5):** Explores freely, asks insightful questions
- **Good (4):** Completes tasks willingly, some exploration
- **Fair (3):** Does tasks but minimal extra effort
- **Poor (2):** Hesitant, limited engagement
- **Very Poor (1):** Disinterested or confused
