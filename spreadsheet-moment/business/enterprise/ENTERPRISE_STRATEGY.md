## Enterprise Sales Process

### Stage 1: Prospecting & Qualification

#### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Company Characteristics:**
- **Size:** 500-5,000 employees
- **Industry:** Technology, Finance, Healthcare, Manufacturing
- **Revenue:** $50M-$5B annual revenue
- **Growth:** 20%+ YoY growth
- **Tech Stack:** Cloud-native, collaboration tools (Slack, Teams)
- **Pain Points:** Data silos, manual reporting, spreadsheet chaos

**Decision Maker Profile:**
- **Title:** CIO, CTO, VP of Engineering, Head of Data
- **Authority:** Budget approval for $50K-$500K
- **Influence:** Technology strategy decisions
- **Pain:** Pressure to modernize data infrastructure

#### Qualification Framework (BANT)

**Budget:**
- Q: "Do you have budget for data collaboration tools?"
- A: Looking for signals (recent budget allocation, competitive displacement)

**Authority:**
- Q: "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- A: Identify economic buyer vs technical buyer vs user buyer

**Need:**
- Q: "What challenges are you facing with current spreadsheet tools?"
- A: Quantify pain (time spent, errors, rework)

**Timeline:**
- Q: "When are you looking to implement a solution?"
- A: Align with fiscal year, project timelines

#### Lead Sources
- **Inbound:** Website demos, product trials, content downloads
- **Outbound:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator, cold email, warm intros
- **Partners:** System integrators, cloud providers (AWS, GCP)
- **Events:** Industry conferences, webinars, roundtables

### Stage 2: Discovery & Needs Analysis

#### Discovery Call Agenda (30 minutes)

**Introduction (5 min)**
- Your role and company
- Their role and background
- Agenda for call
- Time check

**Current State (10 min)**
- Current spreadsheet/workflow tools
- Pain points and challenges
- Team size and collaboration patterns
- Data volume and complexity

**Future State (10 min)**
- Ideal workflow and capabilities
- Success metrics and ROI expectations
- Timeline for implementation
- Budget considerations

**Next Steps (5 min)**
- Qualification assessment
- Proposal next steps
- Stakeholder identification
- Follow-up actions

#### Key Discovery Questions

**Current Tools:**
- "What tools do you currently use for data collaboration?"
- "What do you like/dislike about your current setup?"
- "How many people are using spreadsheets daily?"

**Pain Points:**
- "What's the biggest challenge with your current workflow?"
- "How much time does your team spend on manual data work?"
- "Have you experienced any errors or issues with current tools?"

**Impact:**
- "What would it mean to your team if you could automate that work?"
- "How would faster insights impact your business?"
- "What's the cost of not solving this problem?"

**Decision Process:**
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- "What's your timeline for implementing a solution?"
- "Have you budgeted for this type of solution?"

- **Storytelling:** Use their language and scenarios
- **Interactivity:** Let them drive, not just watch
- **Focus:** Stick to 3-4 key use cases, not everything
- **Value:** Tie features to business value
- **Time:** Respect their time, end on schedule

### Stage 4: Proposal & Negotiation

#### Proposal Components

**Executive Summary**
- Understanding of their challenges
- Proposed solution overview
- Expected ROI and benefits
- Timeline for implementation

**Solution Details**
- Pricing tiers and recommendations
- User count and pricing breakdown
- Implementation plan
- Training and support

**Company Information**
- Spreadsheet Moment overview
- Customer success stories
- Security and compliance
- Support and SLA

**Commercial Terms**
- Pricing and payment terms
- Contract length and renewal
- SLA and support levels
- Legal terms and conditions

#### Pricing Strategy

**Anchor Pricing:**
- Start with list price ($100-150/user/year)
- Volume discounts (20-40% for large deployments)
- Multi-year discounts (additional 10-20%)
- Commitment discounts (prepayment)

**Deal Desk Approval:**
- Discounts >40% require deal desk approval
- Custom terms require legal review
- Payment terms >net 30 require finance approval

**Negotiation Tactics:**
- Focus on value, not price
- Trade price for commitment (term, case study)
- Hold on strategic terms (source code escrow, etc.)
- Walk away point: maintain price integrity

### Stage 5: Procurement & Contracting

#### Procurement Process Navigation

**Stakeholder Mapping:**

**Economic Buyer:**
- Signs the check
- Cares about ROI and business value
- Focus: CFO, VP of Finance, CIO

**Technical Buyer:**
- Evaluates technical fit
- Cares about security, integration, performance
- Focus: CTO, VP of Engineering, Head of Data

**User Buyer:**
- Uses the product daily
- Cares about ease of use, features
- Focus: Managers, individual contributors

**Coach:**
- Internal champion
- Guides us through process
- Provides insights and feedback

#### RFP/RFI Response Process

**RFP Receipt:**
- Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
- Clarify questions and timeline
- Assign proposal team

**Proposal Development:**
- Assign sections to subject matter experts
- Develop win themes and differentiators
- Incorporate customer language
- Review and refine

**Submission:**
- Submit before deadline
- Include executive summary
- Offer follow-up demo

**Follow-up:**
- Schedule Q&A session
- Address clarifying questions
- Request shortlist status

#### Contract Redlining

**Common Redlines:**
- Liability limitations (cap at 1-3x fees)
- Indemnification (mutual, intellectual property)
- Termination (cause vs convenience, notice period)
- Data protection (data ownership, deletion)
- SLA credits (service credits for outages)
- Support response times

**Negotiation Strategy:**
- Collaborative, not adversarial
- Know your walk-away points
- Trade concessions strategically
- Get legal review before signing

## Competitive Selling

### Competitive Positioning

**vs Excel/Office 365:**
- **Our Strength:** AI-first, modern collaboration
- **Their Weakness:** Outdated, weak AI, complex
- **Win Strategy:** Modernize data infrastructure

**vs Google Sheets:**
- **Our Strength:** Advanced AI, better performance
- **Their Weakness:** Limited AI, weak on large datasets
- **Win Strategy:** Power users, advanced use cases

**vs Airtable:**
- **Our Strength:** Powerful calculations, AI
- **Their Weakness:** Expensive, weak calculations
- **Win Strategy:** Better value, more powerful

**vs Smartsheet:**
- **Our Strength:** Modern interface, AI capabilities
- **Their Weakness:** Outdated, expensive
- **Win Strategy:** Modern alternative

### Objection Handling

**"Excel does everything we need"**
- Acknowledge: "Excel is powerful"
- Pivot: "How much time does your team spend on manual work?"
- Solution: "Our AI automates that work, saving 10+ hours/week"

**"Google Sheets is free"**
- Acknowledge: "Google Sheets is great for collaboration"
- Pivot: "What about advanced AI and insights?"
- Solution: "Our AI capabilities are far beyond Google Sheets"

**"We're invested in [competitor]"**
- Acknowledge: "[Competitor] is a solid product"
- Pivot: "What's not working for you?"
- Solution: "We can fill the gaps and provide better ROI"

**"Your company is too young/risky"**
- Acknowledge: "We're a newer company"
- Pivot: "We're well-funded and growing fast"
- Solution: "SOC 2 certified, 99.95% uptime SLA"
