## 1. Definitions and Interpretation

### 1.1 Definitions
In this DPA, the following terms have the meanings set out below:

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **"Controller"** | The entity that determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data. |
| **"Processor"** | The entity that Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. |
| **"Personal Data"** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. |
| **"Processing"** | Any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data. |
| **"Data Subject"** | The natural person to whom Personal Data relates. |
| **"Supervisory Authority"** | A supervisory authority within the meaning of Article 51(1) GDPR. |
| **"GDPR"** | Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council. |
| **"UK GDPR"** | The retained EU law version of the GDPR as incorporated into UK law. |
| **"SCCs"** | Standard Contractual Clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries. |
| **"Data Protection Law"** | GDPR, UK GDPR, and any applicable data protection laws. |
| **"Services"** | The spreadsheet analysis platform services provided by Processor to Controller. |
| **"Security Measures"** | The technical and organizational security measures set out in Annex 2. |

### 1.2 Interpretation
- **Singular Includes Plural:** Words in the singular include the plural and vice versa.
- **Headings:** Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.
- **Schedules and Annexes:** References to schedules and annexes are to the schedules and annexes to this DPA.

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

### 2.1 Status of Parties

#### 2.1.1 Controller Status
The Controller confirms that it is a Controller (or joint controller) of Personal Data processed under this DPA.

#### 2.1.2 Processor Status
The Processor acts as a Processor on behalf of the Controller.

### 2.2 Scope of Processing

#### 2.2.1 Personal Data
The Processor will Process the following Personal Data on behalf of the Controller:

**See Annex 1 (Details of Processing)** for:
- Categories of data subjects
- Categories of Personal Data
- Processing operations
- Purposes of processing

#### 2.2.2 Controller's Instructions
The Processor shall Process Personal Data:
- **Only on documented instructions** from the Controller
- **Unless required to do so** by Union or Member State law to which the Processor is subject
- **In such case** the Processor shall inform the Controller of that legal requirement before Processing, unless that law prohibits such information on important grounds of public interest

### 2.3 Controller's Responsibilities

#### 2.3.1 Compliance with Data Protection Law
The Controller shall:
- **Comply with** all obligations under Data Protection Law relating to the Processing
- **Ensure that** the Processing is lawful
- **Provide** Processor with any information needed to demonstrate compliance
- **Maintain** records of processing activities

#### 2.3.2 Lawful Basis
The Controller shall:
- **Determine and document** the lawful basis for Processing
- **Obtain any necessary consents** from Data Subjects
- **Provide privacy notices** to Data Subjects
- **Comply with** data subject rights

## 3. Processor Obligations

### 3.1 Data Protection Principles
The Processor shall:
- **Process Personal Data** only in accordance with documented instructions
- **Ensure that** persons authorized to Process Personal Data are committed to confidentiality
- **Take appropriate measures** to ensure security of Processing
- **Not engage another processor** without prior specific or general written authorization
- **Assist the Controller** in fulfilling its obligations under Data Protection Law

### 3.2 Documentation
The Processor shall:
- **Maintain a record** of all categories of Processing activities carried out on behalf of the Controller
- **Make that record available** to the Controller and supervisory authorities upon request
- **Maintain records** of documented instructions from the Controller

### 3.3 Compliance
The Processor shall:
- **Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures** to meet Data Protection Law requirements
- **Comply with** the principles of Data Protection by Design and by Default
- **Not alter** Personal Data except as necessary for Processing
- **Not sell** Personal Data or use it for Processor's own purposes

## 4. Data Subject Rights

### 4.1 Assistance with Data Subject Rights
The Processor shall, taking into account the nature of the Processing, assist the Controller by:

#### 4.1.1 Right to be Informed
- **Assisting in providing** privacy notices to Data Subjects
- **Providing information** about Processing activities

#### 4.1.2 Right of Access
- **Assisting in responding** to data subject access requests
- **Providing copies** of Personal Data held

#### 4.1.3 Right to Rectification
- **Assisting in correcting** inaccurate Personal Data
- **Completing incomplete Personal Data**

#### 4.1.4 Right to Erasure
- **Assisting in deleting** Personal Data upon request
- **Where feasible** and taking into account the technology available

#### 4.1.5 Right to Restriction of Processing
- **Assisting in restricting** Processing upon request
- **Where requested** by a Data Subject

#### 4.1.6 Right to Data Portability
- **Assisting in providing** Personal Data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
- **Where requested** by a Data Subject

#### 4.1.7 Right to Object
- **Assisting in responding** to objections to Processing

#### 4.1.8 Rights Related to Automated Decision Making
- **Providing information** about logic involved in automated decision making
- **Providing the significance** and envisaged consequences of such Processing

### 4.2 Process for Handling Rights Requests

#### 4.2.1 Controller's Responsibility
- **Primary Responsibility:** The Controller is primarily responsible for responding to data subject rights requests
- **Processor's Role:** The Processor shall assist as appropriate

#### 4.2.2 Assistance Timeline
- **Acknowledgment:** Within 5 business days of request
- **Response:** As soon as practicable, and no later than the Controller's response deadline

#### 4.2.3 Verification
- The Controller shall verify the identity of the Data Subject making the request

- **Access Controls:** Ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems and services
- **Authorization:** Establish mechanisms to ensure only authorized access

#### 5.1.3 Availability and Resilience

#### 5.1.4 Restoration

- **Testing:** Regular testing and assessment of technical and organizational measures

- Network security
- System security

#### 5.2.2 Organizational Measures
**See Annex 2 (Security Measures)** for detailed organizational measures including:
- Security policies
- Employee training
- Access controls
- Incident response procedures

### 5.3 Compliance with Codes of Conduct

- **Comply with** any approved codes of conduct or certification mechanisms

## 6. Subprocessing

### 6.1 Authorization of Subprocessors

#### 6.1.1 General Authorization
The Controller generally authorizes the Processor to engage subprocessors as set out in Annex 3 (Subprocessors).

#### 6.1.2 Specific Authorization
For subprocessors not listed in Annex 3, the Processor shall:
- **Notify the Controller** in advance of any proposed changes
- **Provide the Controller** with an opportunity to object
- **Allow a reasonable period** (not less than 30 days) for objection

#### 6.1.3 Objection
The Controller may object to the appointment of a subprocessor where:
- The objection is based on **reasonable grounds** relating to data protection
- The objection is made **in writing** within the objection period
- The objection is **specific** to the proposed subprocessor

### 6.2 Subprocessor Obligations

#### 6.2.1 Data Processing Agreement
The Processor shall:
- **Enter into a written agreement** with the subprocessor
- **Impose the same data protection obligations** as set out in this DPA
- **Ensure the subprocessor** provides sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures

#### 6.2.2 Liability
The Processor shall remain **fully liable** to the Controller for the performance of the subprocessor's obligations under this DPA.

### 6.3 Changes to Subprocessors

#### 6.3.1 Notification
The Processor shall:
- **Notify the Controller** of any intended addition or replacement of subprocessors
- **Provide at least 30 days' notice** before engaging a new subprocessor
- **Provide information** about the subprocessor and the nature of the Processing

#### 6.3.2 Objection Period
The Controller may object within 30 days of notification:
- **On reasonable grounds** relating to data protection
- **In writing** to the Processor

#### 6.3.3 Transition
If the Controller objects, the parties shall:
- **Discuss in good faith** the objection and potential alternatives
- **Work to find a solution** that addresses the Controller's concerns
- **If no solution is found** within 60 days, either party may terminate the DPA

## 7. Data Transfers

### 7.1 International Data Transfers

#### 7.1.1 Transfer to Third Countries
The Processor shall not transfer Personal Data to a country outside the EEA or UK unless:
- **The Commission has decided** that the country ensures an adequate level of protection, OR
- **Appropriate safeguards** have been provided, OR
- **Specific derogations** apply

#### 7.1.2 Appropriate Safeguards
Where appropriate safeguards are required, the Processor shall:
- **Use Standard Contractual Clauses** approved by the European Commission
- **Use UK International Data Transfer Agreements** for UK GDPR transfers
- **Implement additional technical and organizational measures** where necessary

#### 7.1.3 Standard Contractual Clauses
The Processor shall:
- **Enter into the Standard Contractual Clauses** (SCCs) with subprocessors in third countries
- **Ensure the SCCs are incorporated** into subprocessor agreements
- **Provide a copy** of the SCCs to the Controller upon request

### 7.2 UK GDPR Transfers
For transfers from the UK to countries outside the UK:
- **Use UK International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs)**
- **Use UK Addendum to EU SCCs**
- **Ensure appropriate safeguards** are in place

### 7.3 Documentation
The Processor shall:
- **Maintain records** of all international data transfers
- **Document the appropriate safeguards** in place
- **Provide information** about transfers to the Controller upon request

### 7.4 Notice of Changes
The Processor shall:
- **Notify the Controller** of any changes that affect international data transfers
- **Provide at least 30 days' notice** of any changes to the safeguards used

## 8. Audit Rights

### 8.1 Right to Audit

#### 8.1.1 Controller's Right
The Controller shall have the right to conduct audits of the Processor's compliance with this DPA.

#### 8.1.2 Audit Frequency
- **Regular Audits:** At least once per year, OR
- **Triggered Audits:** Upon reasonable suspicion of non-compliance

#### 8.1.3 Audit Scope
The audit may cover:
- **Compliance with this DPA**
- **Compliance with documented instructions**
- **Security measures** in place
- **Records of Processing activities**
- **Any other aspect** of the Processor's obligations under this DPA

### 8.2 Audit Process

#### 8.2.1 Notice
- **Advance Notice:** At least 30 days' written notice
- **Audit Plan:** The Controller shall provide an audit plan
- **Reasonable Scope:** The audit scope shall be reasonable and proportionate

#### 8.2.2 Conduct of Audit
- **During Business Hours:** Audits shall be conducted during business hours
- **Minimal Disruption:** The audit shall minimize disruption to Processor's operations
- **Access to Personnel:** The Processor shall provide access to relevant personnel
- **Access to Records:** The Processor shall provide access to relevant records

#### 8.2.3 Audit Report
- **Findings:** The auditor shall provide a report of findings
- **Remediation:** The Processor shall remediate any non-compliance within a reasonable timeframe
- **Verification:** The Controller may verify remediation measures

### 8.3 Audit Expenses

#### 8.3.1 Cost Allocation
- **Controller Pays:** For audits revealing non-compliance by the Processor
- **Processor Pays:** For audits revealing full compliance by the Processor
- **Mutual Agreement:** For audits revealing partial compliance

#### 8.3.2 Travel and Expenses
- **Reasonable Expenses:** Reasonable travel and expenses shall be reimbursed
- **Agreement:** Reimbursement shall be based on prior agreement

### 8.4 Alternative Compliance Verification

#### 8.4.1 Certifications
The Processor may demonstrate compliance through:
- **Certifications:** ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, or equivalent
- **Audit Reports:** Third-party audit reports
- **Assessments:** Security assessments by qualified third parties

#### 8.4.2 Substitution for Audit
Where the Processor provides:
- **Valid Certification:** Current and relevant certification
- **Comprehensive Report:** Detailed third-party audit report
- **Adequate Coverage:** Covering all aspects of this DPA

The Controller may accept such documentation in lieu of a direct audit.

## 9. Data Breach Management

### 9.1 Breach Notification

#### 9.1.1 Notification to Controller
The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach.

#### 9.1.2 Notification Content
The notification shall:
- **Describe the nature** of the Personal Data Breach
- **Include categories** and approximate number of Data Subjects concerned
- **Include categories** and approximate number of Personal Data records concerned
- **Describe the likely consequences** of the Personal Data Breach
- **Describe the measures** taken or proposed to address the Personal Data Breach
- **Include measures** proposed to mitigate adverse effects

#### 9.1.3 Notification Timing
- **Without Undue Delay:** As soon as reasonably practicable, and in any event within 72 hours
- **If Not Within 72 Hours:** Provide reasons for delay

### 9.2 Breach Response

#### 9.2.1 Investigation
The Processor shall:
- **Investigate** the Personal Data Breach
- **Determine the scope** and cause
- **Identify affected Data Subjects** and Personal Data
- **Document findings** of the investigation

#### 9.2.2 Containment
The Processor shall:
- **Take immediate steps** to contain the breach
- **Prevent further unauthorized access** to Personal Data
- **Implement additional security measures** as necessary

#### 9.2.3 Recovery
The Processor shall:
- **Restore access** to Personal Data
- **Repair vulnerabilities** that led to the breach
- **Implement measures** to prevent similar breaches

### 9.3 Breach Records

#### 9.3.1 Record Keeping
The Processor shall:
- **Maintain a record** of all Personal Data Breaches
- **Document the facts** relating to the breach
- **Document the effects** of the breach
- **Document the remedial action** taken

#### 9.3.2 Record Retention
- **Retention Period:** At least 5 years
- **Availability:** Available to Controller and supervisory authorities upon request

### 9.4 Controller's Responsibilities

#### 9.4.1 Notification to Supervisory Authority
The Controller is responsible for:
- **Notifying the supervisory authority** where required by Data Protection Law
- **Within 72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach
- **Unless the breach** is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons

#### 9.4.2 Notification to Data Subjects
The Controller is responsible for:
- **Notifying affected Data Subjects** where required by Data Protection Law
- **Without undue delay** when the breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals

## 11. Deletion or Return of Data

### 11.1 End of Services

#### 11.1.1 Deletion or Return
At the end of the provision of Services relating to Processing:
- **The Processor shall delete or return** all Personal Data to the Controller
- **The Processor shall delete existing copies** unless Union or Member State law requires storage
- **The choice of deletion or return** shall be made by the Controller

#### 11.1.2 Verification
The Processor shall:
- **Provide written confirmation** of deletion or return
- **Maintain records** of deletion or return
- **Provide evidence** of deletion upon request

### 11.2 Backup and Archival Copies

#### 11.2.1 Deletion of Backups
The Processor shall:
- **Delete Personal Data from backups** within a reasonable timeframe
- **Subject to technical constraints** of backup systems
- **Within the retention period** specified in Annex 1

#### 11.2.2 Archival Copies
Where Processor is required to retain Personal Data by law:
- **The Processor shall inform** the Controller of the legal requirement
- **The Processor shall store** such Personal Data securely
- **The Processor shall restrict processing** to storage only

### 11.3 Controller's Responsibility

#### 11.3.1 Backup Data
The Controller is responsible for:
- **Backing up Personal Data** before termination of Services
- **Requesting return of data** if desired

#### 11.3.2 Transition
The Controller shall:
- **Provide reasonable notice** before termination
- **Coordinate with Processor** for orderly transition
- **Retrieve any required data** before deletion

## 12. Term and Termination

### 12.1 Term

#### 12.1.1 Duration
This DPA shall:
- **Commence on** the Effective Date
- **Continue for** the duration of the Services agreement between the parties
- **Remain in effect** until all Personal Data has been deleted or returned

#### 12.1.2 Survival
The following provisions shall survive termination of this DPA:
- Sections 5 (Security Measures)
- Sections 9 (Data Breach Management)
- Section 11 (Deletion or Return of Data)
- Any other provision necessary to give effect to the parties' obligations

### 12.2 Termination

#### 12.2.1 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate this DPA:
- **Upon material breach** by the other party
- **If the breach is not cured** within 30 days of notice
- **Immediately** in case of serious breach involving unauthorized Processing of Personal Data

#### 12.2.2 Termination for Convenience
The Controller may terminate this DPA:
- **Upon 30 days' notice** to the Processor
- **For any reason** or no reason
- **Without penalty**

#### 12.2.3 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:
- **The Processor shall cease Processing** Personal Data
- **The Processor shall delete or return** Personal Data in accordance with Section 11
- **The parties shall cooperate** for orderly transition
- **The Controller shall pay** all fees for Services provided before termination

### 12.3 Transition Assistance

#### 12.3.1 Processor's Obligations
Upon termination, the Processor shall:
- **Assist the Controller** in transitioning to another service provider
- **Provide reasonable assistance** during transition period
- **Respond to reasonable requests** for information

#### 12.3.2 Transition Period
- **Duration:** Up to 90 days after termination
- **Fees:** Controller shall pay reasonable fees for transition assistance
- **Cooperation:** Both parties shall cooperate in good faith

## 13. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

### 13.1 Governing Law

#### 13.1.1 GDPR Jurisdictions
For Personal Data processed in the EEA:
- **This DPA shall be governed by** the laws of the Member State where the Controller is established
- **In relation to obligations** arising under GDPR, the GDPR shall prevail

#### 13.1.2 UK GDPR Jurisdictions
For Personal Data processed in the UK:
- **This DPA shall be governed by** the laws of England and Wales
- **In relation to obligations** arising under UK GDPR, the UK GDPR shall prevail

#### 13.1.3 Other Jurisdictions
For Personal Data processed in other jurisdictions:
- **This DPA shall be governed by** the laws agreed in the Services agreement
- **Subject to mandatory provisions** of applicable data protection laws

### 13.2 Dispute Resolution

#### 13.2.1 Negotiation
The parties shall:
- **Attempt to resolve disputes** through good faith negotiation
- **For a period of at least 30 days** before pursuing other remedies
- **At executive level** of each party

#### 13.2.2 Arbitration (Optional)
If negotiation fails:
- **The dispute may be referred** to arbitration
- **In accordance with** the rules of the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC)
- **Seat of arbitration:** London, England
- **Language:** English

#### 13.2.3 Courts
If arbitration is not chosen:
- **EEA Parties:** Courts of the Member State where the Controller is established
- **UK Parties:** Courts of England and Wales
- **Other Parties:** As specified in the Services agreement

### 13.3 Mandatory Provisions

#### 13.3.1 Data Protection Law
Nothing in this DPA shall:
- **Limit or derogate from** the obligations of the Controller or Processor under Data Protection Law
- **Reduce the level of protection** afforded to Data Subjects under Data Protection Law
- **Prevent supervisory authorities** from exercising their powers

#### 13.3.2 Data Subject Rights
Nothing in this DPA shall:
- **Limit or derogate from** the rights of Data Subjects under Data Protection Law
- **Prevent Data Subjects** from exercising their rights
- **Prevent supervisory authorities** from enforcing Data Subject rights
