## 9. Your Privacy Rights

### 9.1 General Rights
You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

#### 9.1.1 Right to Access
You can request:
- **A copy** of your personal information
- **Details** about how we use your information
- **Information** about who we share your data with
- **How long** we retain your data

#### 9.1.2 Right to Rectification
You can request:
- **Correction** of inaccurate personal information
- **Completion** of incomplete personal information

#### 9.1.3 Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)
You can request deletion of your personal information when:
- It's no longer necessary for the purposes collected
- You withdraw consent (where consent is the legal basis)
- You object to processing (and there's no overriding legitimate interest)
- It was processed unlawfully
- It must be erased to comply with legal obligation

#### 9.1.4 Right to Portability
You can request:
- **A machine-readable copy** of your personal information
- **Transfer** of your data to another service provider

Applies to data:
- Provided by you
- Processed with your consent or contract
- Processed by automated means

#### 9.1.5 Right to Object
You can object to processing based on:
- **Legitimate interests** (unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds)
- **Direct marketing** (absolute right; no exceptions)

#### 9.1.6 Right to Restrict Processing
In certain circumstances, you can request we limit processing when:
- **Accuracy is contested** (while we verify)
- **Processing is unlawful** (but you don't want deletion)
- **No longer needed** (but required for legal claims)
- **Objection is pending** (while we verify legitimate interests)

#### 9.1.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision Making
You have the right:
- **Not to be subject** to solely automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects
- **To express your point of view**
- **To contest the decision**
- **To obtain human intervention**

### 9.2 How to Exercise Your Rights

### 9.4 Third-Party Requests

## 14. Data Breach Procedures

### 14.1 Security Commitment
We take data security seriously and have implemented measures to prevent breaches.

### 14.2 Breach Detection
We monitor for:
- Unauthorized access to personal information
- Accidental destruction, loss, or alteration
- Unauthorized disclosure of personal information

### 14.3 Breach Notification

#### 14.3.1 EU/UK GDPR (72 hours)
If a breach poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will:
- **Notify** the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware
- **Describe** the nature of the breach and categories of data affected
- **Provide** contact details for more information
- **Describe** likely consequences and measures taken to address them

#### 14.3.2 US State Laws (Varies)
- **California:** Within "most expedient time possible, without unreasonable delay"
- **Other States:** Timeframes vary from 30 to 90 days
- **Federal:** No general federal law, but sector-specific requirements

#### 14.3.3 Canada
- **PIPEDA:** Report to Privacy Commissioner and affected individuals when "real risk of significant harm"

#### 14.3.4 Australia
- **Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme:** When eligible data breach occurs

### 14.4 Notification to You
If your personal information is involved in a breach that poses a risk to you, we will:
- **Notify you** via email or in-app notification
- **Describe** what happened
- **Explain** what information was involved
- **Provide** guidance on protective steps
- **Describe** what we're doing to address the breach

### 14.5 Mitigation
When a breach occurs, we will:
- **Immediately secure** affected systems
- **Investigate** the scope and cause
- **Notify** appropriate authorities and affected individuals
- **Implement** measures to prevent recurrence
- **Review** and update security practices
