## 1. What Are Cookies?

### 1.1 Definition
Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit our website. They help us provide you with a better experience by:

- **Remembering your preferences** (e.g., language, region)
- **Understanding how you use our Service** (analytics)
- **Keeping you secure** (authentication, fraud prevention)
- **Delivering relevant content** (personalization)

### 1.2 How Cookies Work
When you visit our website:
1. Our server sends a cookie to your browser
2. Your browser stores the cookie on your device
3. The cookie is sent back to our server on subsequent visits
4. This allows us to recognize your device and remember information

### 1.3 What Cookies Don't Do
Cookies **CANNOT**:
- Access other files on your computer
- Steal personal information
- Spread viruses or malware
- Access your email or contact list (without your permission)

## 3. Types of Cookies We Use

### 3.1 Essential Cookies
**Also known as:** Strictly Necessary Cookies

**Purpose:** Required for the Service to function properly.

**Examples:**
- **Session Cookies:** Maintain your session while you navigate
- **Authentication Cookies:** Keep you logged in
- **Security Cookies:** Protect against CSRF attacks

**Duration:** Session-based (expire when you close your browser) or persistent (expire after a set period)

**Opt-out:** Not available (the Service requires these cookies to function)

**Examples of Essential Cookies We Use:**

| Cookie Name | Purpose | Duration |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| `session_id` | Maintains your login session | Session |
| `csrf_token` | Protects against cross-site request forgery | Session |
| `user_pref` | Remembers essential user preferences | 1 year |

### 3.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies
**Also known as:** Statistics Cookies

**Purpose:** Help us understand how visitors use our Service.

**Examples:**
- **Google Analytics:** Track website usage and performance
- **Amplitude:** User behavior analytics
- **Hotjar:** User interaction tracking and heatmaps

**Duration:** Typically 2 years

**Opt-out:** Available through cookie consent manager or browser settings

**Information Collected:**
- Pages visited and time spent on pages
- Browser type and version
- Operating system
- Screen resolution
- Referring website
- Geographic location (approximate)
- Device type (mobile, desktop, tablet)

**How We Use This Information:**
- Improve website performance
- Identify popular content and features
- Fix technical issues
- Understand user behavior patterns
- Optimize user experience

**Note:** We anonymize IP addresses and do not collect personally identifiable information through these cookies.

**Examples of Analytics Cookies We Use:**

| Cookie Name | Provider | Purpose | Duration |
|-------------|----------|---------|----------|
| `_ga` | Google Analytics | Distinguishes unique users | 2 years |
| `_gid` | Google Analytics | Distinguishes unique users | 24 hours |
| `_gat` | Google Analytics | Throttles request rate | 1 minute |
| `amplitude_id` | Amplitude | User analytics | 10 years |
| `_hjid` | Hotjar | Hotjar ID | 1 year |

### 3.3 Functionality Cookies
**Also known as:** Preference Cookies

**Purpose:** Remember your choices and preferences.

**Examples:**
- **Language Preferences:** Remember your selected language
- **Theme Settings:** Remember light/dark mode preference
- **Layout Settings:** Remember your preferred layout
- **Font Size:** Remember accessibility settings

**Duration:** Typically 1 year

**Opt-out:** Available through account settings or cookie consent manager

**Examples of Functionality Cookies We Use:**

| Cookie Name | Purpose | Duration |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| `language` | Remembers selected language | 1 year |
| `theme` | Remembers theme preference | 1 year |
| `layout` | Remembers layout preference | 1 year |

### 3.4 Targeting and Advertising Cookies
**Also known as:** Marketing Cookies

**Purpose:** Deliver relevant advertisements and measure campaign effectiveness.

**Examples:**
- **Facebook Pixel:** Track ad conversions and build audiences
- **Google Ads:** Track ad performance and remarketing
- **LinkedIn Insight Tag:** Track ad conversions

**Duration:** Varies by provider (typically 1-2 years)

**Opt-out:** Available through cookie consent manager, browser settings, or provider-specific opt-outs

**Information Collected:**
- Pages visited
- Ads clicked
- Actions taken on our website
- Device and browser information
- Geographic location (approximate)

**How We Use This Information:**
- Show relevant ads on our website and third-party sites
- Measure ad campaign effectiveness
- Build custom audiences for targeting
- Exclude users who have taken certain actions

**Examples of Targeting Cookies We Use:**

| Cookie Name | Provider | Purpose | Duration |
|-------------|----------|---------|----------|
| `_fbp` | Facebook | Facebook ad tracking | 3 months |
| `fr` | Facebook | Facebook ad tracking | 3 months |

## 5. Managing Cookies

### 5.1 Browser Settings
You can control and manage cookies through your web browser settings.

#### 5.1.1 Common Browser Settings

**Google Chrome:**
1. Click the three dots in the top-right corner
2. Select "Settings"
3. Click "Privacy and security"
4. Click "Cookies and other site data"
5. Choose your preference:
   - Allow all cookies
   - Block third-party cookies
   - Block all cookies
   - Clear cookies when you close Chrome

**Mozilla Firefox:**
1. Click the menu button (three lines)
2. Select "Settings"
3. Click "Privacy & Security"
4. Under "Cookies and Site Data," choose your preference:
   - Accept cookies and site data
   - Block cookies and site data
   - Customize your settings

**Safari (Desktop):**
1. Click "Safari" in the menu bar
2. Select "Preferences"
3. Click "Privacy"
4. Choose your preference:
   - Block all cookies
   - Prevent cross-site tracking
   - Manage website data

**Safari (iOS/Mobile):**
1. Open Settings
2. Scroll down and tap "Safari"
3. Under "Privacy & Security," choose your preference:
   - Block All Cookies
   - Prevent Cross-Site Tracking

**Microsoft Edge:**
1. Click the three dots in the top-right corner
2. Select "Settings"
3. Click "Cookies and site permissions"
4. Click "Manage and delete cookies and site data"
5. Choose your preference

#### 5.1.2 Limitations of Blocking Cookies
Please be aware that:
- **Blocking all cookies** may prevent the Service from functioning properly
- **Blocking essential cookies** will affect your ability to use the Service
- **Blocking third-party cookies** may affect some features (e.g., social login, analytics)
- **Blocking advertising cookies** will not affect core functionality

### 5.2 Cookie Consent Manager
We provide a cookie consent manager that allows you to:
- **View** all cookies in use
- **Accept or decline** specific cookie categories
- **Withdraw consent** at any time
- **Reset** your cookie preferences

**Access the Cookie Consent Manager:**
- Click the "Cookie Preferences" link in the footer
- Click the "Cookie Settings" button in the cookie banner

### 5.3 Web Browser Plug-ins
You can also use browser plug-ins to manage cookies:
- **Ghostery:** Block tracking technologies
- **Privacy Badger:** Block invisible trackers
- **uBlock Origin:** Block ads and trackers
- **Disconnect:** Block tracking services

### 5.4 Do Not Track (DNT)
Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" signal to indicate your preference not to be tracked.

**Our Policy:**
- We respect the Do Not Track signal in the following jurisdictions:
  - California (under CCPA)
  - European Union (under GDPR)
  - Other jurisdictions with DNT requirements

- We do not track users for advertising purposes when DNT is enabled in these jurisdictions.

When you visit our website for the first time, you will see a cookie consent banner that:
- **Informs** you about our use of cookies
- **Provides** a link to this Cookie Policy
- **Offers** choices to accept or decline cookies
- **Allows** you to manage cookie preferences

### 6.2 Consent Options
Our cookie consent banner offers the following options:

#### 6.2.1 Accept All
- Accepts all cookies, including essential, analytics, functionality, and targeting cookies
- Stores your preference for future visits
- Allows us to provide you with the full experience

#### 6.2.2 Reject All (or Accept Essential Only)
- Accepts only essential cookies
- Rejects all non-essential cookies (analytics, functionality, targeting)
- Allows you to use the Service with minimum tracking

#### 6.2.3 Manage Preferences
- Opens the Cookie Consent Manager
- Allows you to customize your cookie preferences
- Choose which cookie categories to accept or decline

### 6.3 Granular Consent
We provide granular consent options for:
- **Analytics Cookies:** Opt-in or opt-out
- **Functionality Cookies:** Opt-in or opt-out
- **Targeting Cookies:** Opt-in or opt-out

**Note:** Essential cookies cannot be declined as the Service requires them.

### 6.4 Implied Consent
**What happens if you continue without making a choice?**

- **In the EU/UK:** We require explicit consent for non-essential cookies. We do not rely on implied consent.
- **In other jurisdictions:** Continuing to use the Service may be considered implied consent for cookies that are not essential.
- **Recommended:** Make an explicit choice to ensure your preferences are respected.

### 6.5 Withdrawing Consent
You can withdraw your consent at any time:
- **Cookie Consent Manager:** Update your preferences
- **Browser Settings:** Block or delete cookies
- **Clearing Cookies:** Clear cookies from your browser

**Effect:** Withdrawing consent will stop future use of cookies, but does not affect cookies already placed.

### 6.6 Consent Records
We maintain records of:
- When consent was given
- What you consented to
- When consent was withdrawn
- Information provided to you about cookies

## 11. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Cookie** | Small text file stored on your device |
| **First-Party Cookie** | Cookie set by the website you are visiting |
| **Third-Party Cookie** | Cookie set by a service other than the website you are visiting |
| **Session Cookie** | Cookie that expires when you close your browser |
| **Persistent Cookie** | Cookie that remains on your device for a set period |
| **Pixel Tag** | Small transparent image used to track email opens and website visits |
| **Web Beacon** | Similar to a pixel tag; used to track user behavior |
| **LocalStorage** | Web storage that stores data with no expiration date |
| **SessionStorage** | Web storage that stores data for the duration of your session |
| **Do Not Track (DNT)** | Browser setting that signals your preference not to be tracked |
| **GDPR** | General Data Protection Regulation (EU) |
| **CCPA** | California Consumer Privacy Act |
| **ePrivacy Directive** | EU directive on privacy and electronic communications |
